## Devotional

Sometimes sentences in the scripture make you read it more than onece. One of those for me is, "Although he was a son, he learned obedience through what he suffered" (Hebrews 5:8). Jesus learned obedience? The sinless Son of God, the One who never once told the Father no, had something to learn? Let me say it carefully so we don't get confused. Jesus did not learn how to obey, because He never lacked the willingness. He learned what obedience involves, and He learned it the only way obedience can be learned, by actually walking through what the Father asked of Him.

Think it through with me. Can a man call himself obedient if nothing has ever been asked of him? Can you claim endurance if you have never carried weight? Can you claim faithfulness if you have never once been tempted to quit? Obedience requires opposition. It requires a hardship pressing against your will, forcing your will to choose, and that choosing under pressure is the learning. Like I said Sunday in regards to swimming, a man floating on his back can say he is obeying the law of buoyancy, and he is, but he has not faced the resistance of the water yet. He has not learned to streamline, to push against what pushes back. Every hardship the water offers teaches a deeper obedience to its laws, and the swimmer who has faced the most resistance is the one who moves through it best. Jesus went all the way down into the resistance. Hebrews 2:9 says He tasted death itself.

Now look at what the suffering produced. It was fitting that God, "in bringing many sons to glory, should make the founder of their salvation perfect through suffering" (Hebrews 2:10). Perfect there does not mean He was ever flawed. The word carries the idea of being made complete, fully qualified. Nobody was better suited to save sufferers than the One who had suffered everything, and so His emptying qualified Him as our high priest, "the source of eternal salvation to all who obey him" (Hebrews 5:9). John Calvin said, "Christ did this to give us the pattern of His own submission, and that if we want the obedience of Christ to be of any advantage to us, we must copy it." 

"If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me" (Luke 9:23).
His example is His expectation.

So lean in, because you might really need to hear it. Some of you are empty right now because God is qualifying you for something you cannot yet see. The assignment ahead of you requires an obedience that can only be learned in the pouring (emptying of the vessel), a depth that floating on your back could never teach you. The resistance you have been begging God to remove may be the very classroom, trial or storm He is using to make you ready.

**God may not call the qualified, but He qualifies through the emptying.**

## Application

1. Where have you been treating resistance as proof God is against you, when it may be the exact training He is using to qualify you?
2. What is the one hard thing God has asked of you that you keep circling instead of walking through, and what would obedience look like there this week?

## Challenge

Name the resistance you are in right now, the pressure you keep asking God to take away. Then ask a different prayer today and ask Him what this resistance is teaching you to obey, and what assignment it might be qualifying you for. Write down what comes, even if it is only a guess, and receive it in obedience.

## Prayer

Father, I confess I have wanted the qualification without the emptying, the depth without the resistance. Thank You that Jesus went all the way down into the water I am afraid of, that He tasted death itself and came out the fully qualified source of my salvation. If this empty season is Your classroom, then keep me in it until the lesson is learned. Teach my will to choose You under pressure, the way His did. In Jesus' name, amen.
