## Devotional

Follow Joseph's resume for a minute. Thrown in a pit by his own brothers. Sold as a slave. He runs Potiphar's house with integrity, and when Potiphar's wife grabs him, listen to his answer. "How then can I do this great wickedness and sin against God?" (Genesis 39:9). That is obedience under pressure. And what did his obedience buy him? Prison. Doing right made his suffering worse, not better. Peter says there is a whole category for that, suffering for doing good (1 Peter 2:20), and Joseph lived in that category for years. He was never disobedient, and he was never comfortable. So let me put the question to you the way I had to put it to myself. Are you able to remain obedient without being comfortable?

Now watch how the psalmist reads those years, because this might be the key for somebody today. "His feet were hurt with fetters; his neck was put in a collar of iron; until what he had said came to pass, the word of the Lord tested him" (Psalm 105:18-19). The word of the Lord tested him. The suffering was the classroom, same as it was for Jesus. And underline that word until, because it means the testing had a horizon. It ran until the word was fulfilled, and then it stopped. Joseph's emptying had an end date on God's calendar even when it had no end in Joseph's sight.

And why the testing at all? Because of what was coming. In Genesis 41, Pharaoh lifts Joseph from the prison to the palace in a single day and sets him over all Egypt. Think about what that kind of power does to an untested thirty-year-old. Power finds the cracks in a man. Promotion has ruined more people than prison ever did. The pit, the slavery, the false accusation, the forgotten years, that was the forge where God built a man who could hold the second throne of Egypt without it destroying him. The years were never detours around the promotion. They were the road to it. And when Joseph finally stands in front of the brothers who started it all, the maturity does the talking. "You meant evil against me, but God meant it for good" (Genesis 50:20). Two authors, one event. The suffering was inside the will of God the whole time.

One caution before you run ahead, because God does not always attach a palace to the testing. Hebrews 11 tells of saints tested every bit as faithfully as Joseph who stayed destitute, afflicted, mistreated to the end, and Scripture's verdict is that the world was not worthy of them (Hebrews 11:36-38). The guaranteed fruit of the testing is Christlike maturity (Hebrews 12:11). Sometimes God adds an earthly promotion to it, and sometimes the maturity is the promotion. So don't obey for the reward. Obey for the righteousness.

**God matures you in the emptying so the assignment doesn't ruin you in the filling.**

## Application

1. If God handed you the thing you have been praying for today, what untested crack in you would the weight of it find?
2. Where have you been obeying for the reward instead of the righteousness, and what would it look like to keep obeying if the reward never came?

## Challenge

Write down the one place your season feels endless, the fetters and the iron collar of it. Next to it, write the word until, and under that, Psalm 105:19. Then ask God for faithfulness inside the classroom instead of an early dismissal from it, because the testing has a horizon even when you cannot see one.

## Prayer

Lord, You know the years that feel forgotten, the seasons where doing right seemed to make everything harder. Thank You that the word tested Joseph until, that Your calendar had an end date his eyes could not find. Forge in me now what the coming weight will require, and if the promotion never looks like a palace, let the maturity be enough. Teach me to obey for the righteousness and not the reward. In Jesus' name, amen.
