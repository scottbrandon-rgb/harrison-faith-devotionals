## Devotional

Put the two chapters side by side, because the distance between them is one page. In 1 Kings 18, Elijah stands alone on Mount Carmel against four hundred and fifty prophets of Baal, calls down fire from heaven, turns a nation's heart in an afternoon, and outruns Ahab's chariot on the strength of the Spirit. That is costly, public, complete obedience, the assignment seen through to the very end. One chapter later he is under a broom tree asking God to take his life. He did not backslide between those chapters. He emptied. He spent everything God gave him finishing the thing God asked of him.

Now keep two things separate here, because Elijah failed at it and so do we. Depletion and despair are two different things. The depletion was honorable. He drained the tank completing the task, and that kind of emptiness is the receipt for a job finished. But the despair, "take my life, I'm the only one left," that was the lie his feelings layered on top of the emptiness. The exhaustion was real. The interpretation was false. Am I talking to somebody? You finished the thing God asked of you, and instead of a victory lap you got a broom tree, and your feelings have been testifying against you ever since. God is finished with me. I'm alone in this.

So watch what God actually does with His empty prophet, because there is not one word of rebuke in it. Food under the broom tree. Water. Sleep. A low whisper instead of a lecture. Then a fact that dismantles the lie, "I will leave seven thousand in Israel, all the knees that have not bowed to Baal" (1 Kings 19:18). And then the word that reframes the whole broom tree, "Go, return on your way" (1 Kings 19:15). Go anoint Hazael. Go anoint Jehu. Go anoint Elisha. Elijah thought he was at a period, and God handed him a comma. Notice something tender in it, church. God never tells Elijah "well done." He shows it, by provisioning him and re-sending him. If you have been provisioned and re-sent, you have been commended, whether you ever heard the words or not.

I owe you one honest caveat before we land. Some emptiness follows disobedience, ask Jonah, emptied out in the belly of a fish because he ran. If that is you, the road back starts with naming the running instead of renaming it, and take heart, because the same God who transitions Elijahs redeems Jonahs. But for the rest of you, the poured-out ones, Peter says it plainly, "if when you do good and suffer for it you endure, this is a gracious thing in the sight of God. For to this you have been called" (1 Peter 2:20-21).

And now hear how the anchor chapter ends, because this is where the whole week has been walking. Philippians 2:8 says He emptied Himself and humbled Himself to the point of death. Verse 9 says, "Therefore God has highly exalted him." The emptied One is the exalted One. The cup that got poured out is the cup God lifted highest. Your emptiness is not a permanent state, only a painful one, and in the hands of the Father it was never evidence of failure. The cup is empty because the offering was made.

**The cup is empty because the offering was made.**

## Application

1. Which voice is actually speaking over your season right now, the honorable depletion or the lying despair, and can you tell the difference yet?
2. What provision has God already set beside your broom tree, rest, people, His Word, that you have been too discouraged to receive?

## Challenge

Name your empty season out loud to the Lord today. Name the obedience, or the disobedience, that came before it. Then ask Him the question this week has been building toward, "Father, are You qualifying me, maturing me, or moving me?" And whatever He shows you, walk in it.

## Prayer

Father, thank You that You meet Your empty servants with bread and not lectures, with a whisper and not a verdict. Where my feelings have been testifying against me, answer them with the truth, that I am not alone and You are not finished. If I am under the broom tree, provision me and send me again, because being re-sent by You is all the commendation I need. And thank You that the emptied One is the exalted One, and my poured-out cup is safe in His hands. In Jesus' name, amen.
