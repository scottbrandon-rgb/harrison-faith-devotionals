## Devotional

Grief has a gravity to it. Left alone it pulls everything down and in, and before long you are not praying anymore, you are just narrating your own sinking to whoever will listen. Watch how David breaks that gravity in verse 3. The same mouth that was cataloging his misery turns and says, "Consider and answer me, O Lord my God" (Psalm 13:3). He stops talking about his despair and starts talking to his God. That turn is the whole ballgame.

Despair talks about God. Lament talks to God. It is the difference between the man who vents about his marriage to everyone but his wife, and the one who finally sits down across the table and says the hard thing to the person who can actually do something about it. When you're empty, the enemy does not mind you talking about God all day long. He minds terribly when you turn and talk to Him.

Now look at what David asks for first, because it is not what you would expect. He does not ask for new circumstances. He does not ask for his enemies to be removed. He asks for light. "Light up my eyes" (Psalm 13:3). David is wise enough to know his vision has gone dark, that his feelings have stopped telling him the truth about God, so before he begs God to change anything around him, he begs God to change what he can see. Paul prayed the very same thing over the church, "having the eyes of your hearts enlightened, that you may know what is the hope to which he has called you" (Ephesians 1:18).

There is nothing wrong with asking God for the exit. David asks for deliverance too, further down the psalm. But he shows us an order, and the order matters. Ask for eyes before you ask for exits. When you are lost in the wilderness the first thing you need is not a way out, it is the ability to see, because a man who can see is a man who can walk. Most of us pray the season away before we ever let God show us one thing inside it. We want the tunnel to end. God, more often, wants to give us eyes in the dark.

So here is the honest question for your empty season. Is your first prayer for the season to end, or for your eyes to open? I'm not asking because the answer is easy. I'm asking because the season may not lift today, and if it doesn't, you are going to need something better than an exit. You are going to need sight. Perspective is the first provision God hands out in the wilderness, and He has never once run short of it.

**Ask for eyes before you ask for exits.**

## Application

1. When you finally pray in a hard season, are you talking to God, or just talking about Him where He can hear you?
2. What would it look like to ask God to light up your eyes today, before you ask Him to change a single circumstance?

## Challenge

Before you ask God to fix anything today, ask Him for sight. Pray verse 3 as it stands, "light up my eyes," and then name one place in your dark season where you have been letting your feelings tell you the truth about God. Ask Him to show you what is actually there, not what the fear insists is there. Sight comes before the exit.

## Prayer

Father, I have spent this season talking about You more than talking to You, and my eyes have gone dark. Before You change anything around me, change what I can see. Light up my eyes the way You did David's, so I can tell the difference between what is true and what I only feel. I still want the way out, Lord, but today I'm asking first for the sight to walk while I wait. In Jesus' name, amen.
