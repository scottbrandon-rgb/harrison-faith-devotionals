## Devotional

Some of you did everything right and the tank still ran dry. You prayed and obeyed and stayed faithful when it cost you something, and God still felt a thousand miles away. I'm not going to hand you a verse to make that feeling behave. I want to sit in it with you for a minute, because David sat in it, and God left his words in our Bible on purpose.

Read how his psalm opens. "How long, O Lord? Will you forget me forever? How long will you hide your face from me?" (Psalm 13:1). Four times in two verses David asks how long. This is not a man with neat, tidy faith. This is the man God Himself called a man after His own heart, and he is asking God to His face why He has gone silent.

Sit on that phrase, the hiding of God's face, because it is not a poem. Back in Numbers 6 the priests blessed the people with it, "the Lord make his face to shine upon you... and give you peace" (Numbers 6:25-26). The face of God was the sign of His nearness, the felt sense that He was for you. So when David says God has hidden His face, he means the one thing that keeps him and blesses him and gives him peace feels gone. If you know that feeling in your body right now, you are not faithless and you are not crazy. You are in the Psalms.

Here is where most of us go wrong, and I have gone wrong both ways. Some of us pick denial, so we stuff it down and distract ourselves and tell everyone at church we're fine while we're bleeding out under the pew. Others pick despair, so we feel the ache and decide it must mean we were never really His, and we quietly let go of the rope. Denial says a real Christian would never feel this. Despair says because I feel this I must not be a real Christian. Both are lying to you.

There is a third door, and Scripture has a name for it. It's called lament. A lament is a prayer in pain that keeps talking to God instead of about Him. Denial buries the pain. Despair buries the hope. Lament carries both to God and refuses to go quiet. So let me ask you what I had to ask myself in this last season. When you run empty, do you go silent toward God, or do you get honest with Him? David got honest, four times honest, and that honesty was not the death of his faith. It was his faith, refusing to stop talking to the only One who could do anything about it.

**Lament is your faith refusing to go silent.**

## Application

1. When the ache comes, which door do you reach for first, denial or despair, and what does that reveal about what you think God can handle?
2. What is the one thing you keep telling God you're fine about when you are not, and what would it look like to say it to Him honestly today?

## Challenge

Find ten quiet minutes today and pray your own version of Psalm 13:1 out loud. Don't clean it up first. Tell God how long it has felt, where He has seemed hidden, and what you're afraid He has forgotten, in your own plain words. Honesty is not the opposite of faith. It is the proof you still believe He is listening.

## Prayer

Lord, I have gone quiet toward You when I should have been honest with You. I called it faith, but really I was afraid of what I might say if I opened my mouth. How long, God. You already know the rest of that sentence better than I do. Teach me to carry the ache to You instead of burying it the way David did, and hold onto me while I learn. In Jesus' name, amen.
