## Devotional

Maybe you have made it this far in the week and you can say it now. I can turn to God. I can even trust Him in the dark. But you still have one question left, and it is the hardest one of all. Why the season at all? Why would a good Father let His own child run this empty for this long? David never answers that one. Paul does, because Paul went down into that pit himself.

Paul says he was "so utterly burdened beyond our strength that we despaired of life itself" (2 Corinthians 1:8). Read that again, because we sand the edges off of Paul too easily. The apostle who wrote half the New Testament despaired of life itself. Then he tells us why God allowed it. "But that was to make us rely not on ourselves but on God who raises the dead" (2 Corinthians 1:9). Some of us will not lean our full weight on God until He is the only thing left to lean on. The empty season is where He becomes the floor, because everything else we were standing on is gone. That is the first purpose. Reliance.

There is a second purpose, and it is the reason your pain was never only about you. God "comforts us in all our affliction, so that we may be able to comfort those who are in any affliction, with the comfort with which we ourselves are comforted by God" (2 Corinthians 1:3-4). Comfort was never meant to stop with you. It runs downhill. What God pours into you at 2am in your worst week becomes the very thing you hand to someone else in theirs. The valley you are walking is not wasted. It is teaching you to sit with hurting people the way the polished and the untouched never could.

I will tell you something from Sunday. I stood up in front of our church and admitted I am in a hard place too, that your pastor runs empty like anyone else. And when the service ended, you came up and prayed over me. I need you to understand what happened in that moment, because it is the whole point. I was not the pastor praying for the church right then. I was a member of the body being carried by it. That is what a body does. It bears one another's burdens (Galatians 6:2), and the burden runs both directions. I am blessed to belong to you, not only to lead you.

So hear the whole week in one breath. A hidden face is not an absent God, and an empty season is never a wasted one. God is spending your dark on something. He is teaching you to rely on the One who raises the dead, and He is filling you with a comfort you will pour out on somebody else before this year is done. In the hands of the Father of mercies, the emptiest stretch of your life may turn out to be the beginning of your usefulness and not the end of it. Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, the God of all comfort, who has never once wasted a wound.

**A hidden face is not an absent God, and an empty season is never a wasted one.**

## Application

1. Where have you been reading your empty season as proof you are useless to God, instead of the place He is making you useful in a way comfort never could?
2. Who is one person walking through something you have already survived, and what would it look like to hand them the comfort God gave you this week?

## Challenge

Think of one person carrying something you have carried before. Reach out to them today, not with advice, with presence. Tell them the honest truth about your own hard season and the God who met you in it. The comfort you received was never only for you, so spend some of it before the week is out.

## Prayer

Father of mercies, I have been asking why this season, and I may not get the full answer this side of heaven. But teach me to rely on You the way Paul learned to, as the God who raises the dead when everything else has already died. Take even this emptiness and waste none of it. Fill me with a comfort I can carry to someone else, and thank You for a body that carries me when I cannot stand on my own. You have never once wasted a wound. In Jesus' name, amen.
