## Devotional

You might expect the story to say that when the shepherd found the sheep and hoisted it onto his shoulders, he was exhausted. Frustrated. Bitter about the cost. That would make sense, wouldn't it? After all the terrain, the cliffs, the ravines, the wolves, the hours of searching through rough country for one animal that got itself into this mess... you'd expect some resentment. But that's not what scripture says. It says he laid it on his shoulders, rejoicing. And when he got home, he called together his friends and neighbors and said, "Rejoice with me, for I have found my sheep that was lost." The hardest day of the shepherd's life was also his happiest, because the search was never about the cost. It was about the sheep.

Hebrews tells us exactly what was in Christ's heart on the way to the cross. It says He endured it for the joy set before Him. The worst death in human history, the most agonizing and humiliating way a person could die, and He called it His joy. Not because the suffering was pleasant, but because He knew what was waiting on the other side of it. He knew that when it was finished, He could look around the fold and count again. Ninety-seven. Ninety-eight. Ninety-nine. One hundred. That's what the cross was about. Not just judgment, not just atonement, but a shepherd who refused to leave you out there and was willing to pay whatever it cost to bring you home.

In Luke 15:7 Jesus says there is more joy in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who need no repentance. So right now, heaven is not waiting with crossed arms and a clipboard. Heaven is waiting with a party. The angels are not standing around wondering if you're worth the trouble. They are ready to celebrate the moment you come home. And what I need you to hear is that "lost" is not a permanent label, it's a temporary condition, and it can change today. You didn't initiate the search and you can't carry yourself home, but there is one thing you can do. You can surrender. You can stop hiding behind the rocks, stop blending into the terrain, stop pretending that your lostness is normal, and say, "Here I am, Shepherd. Find me."

Isaiah 53 says He was pierced for our transgressions, crushed for our iniquities, and upon Him was the chastisement that brought us peace. He didn't just find you. He healed you. He didn't just carry you home. He restored you. Not because you earned it, not because you figured it out, but because that's who He is. He is the good shepherd who lays down His life for the sheep. So the only question left is this: will you let Him pick you up? Because He already left the ninety-nine. He already walked the rough path. He already paid the price. And He is standing right in front of you this morning with His arms open, ready to take you home rejoicing.

## Application

1. What would it look like for you to stop resisting the shepherd's rescue and fully surrender to Him today, not just in word but in the way you live this week?

2. How does knowing that your homecoming produces joy in heaven reshape the way you see your own value to God?

## Challenge

Today is the day. Write out a prayer of surrender in your own words. Not a formula, not a script, just an honest conversation with the shepherd. Tell Him where you've been, where you are, and that you're ready to let Him carry you home. Then share it with one person, a pastor, a friend, someone who can walk this next season with you. Because the shepherd doesn't just find you. He brings you back to a fold, to a people, to a family.

## Prayer

Father, I hear You counting and I know I've been missing. I have wandered, I have normalized my lostness, I have tried to fight battles I was never equipped to win, and I have tried to walk myself home when I didn't have the strength to stand. But today I surrender. I stop running, I stop hiding, I stop pretending. Here I am, Shepherd. Find me. Pick me up. Carry me wherever You want me to go because I trust that You endured the cross for the joy of bringing me home. Let my life from this day forward be evidence that I was found. In Jesus' name, amen.
