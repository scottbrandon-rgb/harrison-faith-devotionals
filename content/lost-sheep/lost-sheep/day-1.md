## Devotional

I want you to imagine something with me. The sun is going down, the day is winding to a close, and a shepherd is doing what shepherds do at the end of every single day. He counts. One, two, three... all the way up. Ninety-seven, ninety-eight, ninety-nine. And he stops. Because there should be one more. So he counts again, just to be sure. Ninety-seven, ninety-eight, ninety-nine. Someone is missing.

Now, the shepherd doesn't count because it's routine, he counts because every single one matters to him. He knows them. He knows their names, knows their habits, knows which one likes to wander toward the ridge and which one lags behind. And when the number comes up short, he doesn't shrug it off and say "ninety-nine is close enough." No, the absence of one creates an urgency in him that ninety-nine safe sheep cannot settle. That missing one may not know it's gone, but the shepherd knows, and it bothers him. Psalm 139 tells us that God's thoughts toward us outnumber the grains of sand. You are not a statistic to Him. You are a name, and when He looks around the fold and you are not there, He feels it.

So think about your life for just a moment. Maybe you've been going through the motions, keeping up the rhythms, paying the bills, showing up where you're supposed to show up, and you feel fine. Life feels manageable. You might even say things are pretty good. But what if the reason you feel fine is not because you are fine, but rather because you've drifted so gradually you didn't notice the distance? Sheep don't wander away all at once. They graze. One patch of grass leads to another, and before they know it the fold is nowhere in sight. That's how drifting works in your life too, because it doesn't announce itself. You don't wake up one morning and decide to be far from God. You just follow one thing that looked interesting, then another, and then you look up and the shepherd's voice sounds faint.

But here is the incredible thing about this passage. Jesus doesn't tell this parable to scold the wandering sheep. He tells it to reveal the heart of the shepherd. Before you ever thought about coming home, He was already thinking about coming to find you. That's where this week begins. Not with your effort to get back, not with your plan to fix things, but rather with a shepherd who counts and realizes you are missing. And tomorrow, we're going to talk about something that might be a little uncomfortable... because the hardest part of being lost is that most of the time, you don't even know it.

## Application

1. When was the last time you sensed the closeness of the shepherd's voice in your life, and what has changed between then and now?

2. Is there an area where you have been gradually drifting without realizing it, where "close enough" has quietly replaced real closeness with God?

## Challenge

Take five minutes today, away from your phone and away from the noise, and ask the Lord one honest question: "Am I where you want me to be?" Don't try to answer it yourself. Just sit in the silence and listen for His voice. The shepherd is counting, and He already knows where you are.

## Prayer

Father, I confess that sometimes I don't even realize how far I've drifted. Life keeps moving and I keep going, and somewhere along the way I stopped listening for Your voice. But today I am grateful that You are still counting. You know my name and You know where I am even when I don't. Open my ears to hear You again. Remind me that I am not just another number in a crowd, but that I am known and pursued by a shepherd who refuses to settle for ninety-nine. Begin that work in my heart this week. In Jesus' name, amen.
