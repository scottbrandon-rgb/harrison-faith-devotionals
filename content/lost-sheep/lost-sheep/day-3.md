## Devotional

If you've ever spent any time around actual sheep, you know something pretty fast: they cannot fight. They can't bite, they can't kick, they can't scratch, they can't threaten. They have zero capacity to defend themselves against anything. And do you know what a sheep does when it senses danger? It huddles together with other sheep. That's it. That's the whole plan. A group of defenseless animals pressing close and hoping the threat passes. And when a sheep is alone, when it's the one that wandered off, it doesn't even have the comfort of the huddle. It just... freezes. It cowers. Its true nature is meek, and under the weight of threat it is completely and utterly crushed.

Now, I'm not really talking about sheep this morning. I'm talking about you. I'm talking about what happens when the enemy attacks and you've been living outside the shepherd's protection. Maybe it was a phone call that leveled you, a diagnosis you didn't see coming, the moment she said she was leaving or he said he had somebody else. And all of a sudden, overnight, you had no more fight in you because your true nature surfaced. You became speechless, lifeless, stuck. You felt it in your chest and you didn't know what to do, where to go, or who to call. Because when a sheep is separated from the shepherd, it has no resources to fight. None. Jesus looked at the crowds and saw exactly that. He saw people who were harassed and helpless. Beaten down by the enemy, overwhelmed by life, and completely without the ability to save themselves. And His response was not disappointment. His response was compassion.

Can I just say this... we live in a culture that tells you the opposite of what scripture tells you. The culture says pull yourself up by the bootstraps, read the right book, say the right affirmation, practice the right morning routine, and you can fix whatever is broken in your life. But Paul said in Romans 3 that no one is righteous, no one understands, no one seeks God. And I think some of us have been exhausting ourselves trying to do what only the shepherd can do. You've been trying to fight a battle you were never equipped to fight on your own. You've been trying to push through pain that was designed to push you toward a rescuer, not through a self-help plan. Genesis 4 says that sin is crouching at your door, and it longs to master you. The enemy knows you're resourceless apart from the shepherd, and he is counting on you trying to handle it alone.

Phew. But here's the hope. Ezekiel 34 says, "I myself will search for my sheep and will seek them out." The shepherd sees your helplessness and he doesn't wait for you to get it together. He moves toward you. And tomorrow, we're going to see just how far he's willing to go... because when he finds you, he doesn't hand you a map home. He picks you up.

## Application

1. Where in your life have you been exhausting yourself trying to fix something that only the shepherd has the power to restore?

2. What would it look like today to stop fighting in your own strength and admit to God that you are defenseless without Him?

## Challenge

Identify one battle you have been fighting alone, whether it's a habit, a hurt, a fear, or a failure you keep cycling back to. Tell someone you trust about it today. Not to fix it, but just to say it out loud: "I can't do this on my own." That admission is not weakness. It's the first honest thing the sheep has said in a long time.

## Prayer

Father, I have been acting like I can handle this on my own, and I can't. I have been telling myself that I just need a little more effort, a little more discipline, a little more time, and I'll figure it out. But the truth is I am defenseless without You. I have no resources to fight the enemy on my own. I am harassed and I am helpless, and I need a shepherd. Thank You that Your response to my helplessness is not frustration but rather compassion. Come find me in this place where I have been pretending to be strong. I'm done pretending. In Jesus' name, amen.
