## Devotional

Forty years. Forty years of walking. Forty years of funerals. Forty years of a generation dying in the sand because they would not obey the voice of the Lord.

And how do we know they would not obey? Read the wilderness chapters. They complained. They murmured. They griped at Moses, they griped at God, they griped at the menu, they griped at the leadership, they griped at the timeline. The Red Sea was barely dry on their feet and they were already wishing for Egypt.

Here is what I want you to write down somewhere you will see it this week. What you complain about is what you have failed to surrender.

That sentence will preach to you for the rest of your life. Read it again. What you complain about is what you have failed to surrender. The thing that keeps coming out of your mouth as a grievance is the thing your heart is still holding onto. You are not complaining because the situation is uniquely hard. You are complaining because you have not yet bowed your neck to the Lord about it.

Paul says it plain in Philippians 2:14, "Do all things without grumbling or disputing" (ESV). Why? The next verse tells you. So that you may be blameless and innocent, children of God without blemish in a crooked and twisted generation. Complaining is not a personality trait. It is not a coping mechanism. It is a witness problem. It tells the watching world that the God you claim to trust is not actually trustworthy with this part of your life.

Hear me, church. Israel complained in Egypt about the bricks. They complained in the wilderness about the food. They complained at Kadesh about the giants. Same people. New scenery. Same mouth. The geography changed and the heart did not. That is the warning. You can cross your Jordan and still carry your Egypt in your mouth.

Can I just say this. The Lord is not afraid of your honesty. Lament is in the Bible. The Psalms are full of hard prayers. There is a difference between bringing your pain to God and grumbling about God to other people. One drives you to Him. The other drives you away from Him and poisons everyone in earshot.

So today is an audit day. Listen to yourself. What words keep coming out? About your spouse. About your kids. About your boss. About the church. About your body. About the money. About the season. The thing you cannot stop talking about is the thing you have not yet handed over. The complaint is the receipt. It is proof that you are still holding the bag.

And here is the hard part. The complaint usually has a comfort hiding inside it. We complained about Egypt because Egypt was familiar. We complain about our current bondage because letting go of it would mean trusting God for what is next. Tomorrow we will look at where some of this came from in the first place, because for a lot of us, the complaining mouth was handed down before it was ever chosen.

## Application

1. Write down the three things you have complained about most in the last two weeks. What does each one say about what you have not yet surrendered to the Lord?
2. Pick one of those three. Confess it as unbelief, by name, and ask the Lord what obedient step He is asking you to take instead of grumbling.

## Challenge

Try a complaint fast for the next 24 hours. Every time a grievance starts to form in your mouth, stop and turn it into a sentence of prayer. Same subject. New direction. At the end of the day, write down what God showed you about your heart in those moments you almost spoke and did not.

## Prayer

Father, I have been calling my unbelief by other names. I have called it venting. I have called it processing. I have called it being honest. You are calling it stubbornness. You are calling it the same heart Israel carried out of Egypt and into the sand. Forgive me. Put a guard on my mouth and a knife to my heart. Cut out the complaint. Give me back the joy of freedom. I do not want to die in the wilderness still wishing for the brickyard. In Jesus' name, Amen.
