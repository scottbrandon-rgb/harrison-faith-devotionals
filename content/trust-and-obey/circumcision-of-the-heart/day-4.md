## Devotional

The Hebrew word behind the name Gilgal is galal. It means to roll. The Lord rolled something away that day, and Israel named the spot for it. They did not name it for the battle they had not yet fought. They did not name it for the river they had just crossed. They named it for the moment the Lord said, "Today I have rolled away the reproach of Egypt from you."

Stop and let that word "today" do its work.

Not eventually. Not after you clean yourself up. Not after a few more years of trying harder. Today. The Lord set a time stamp on it. He rolled the shame off His people in one declaration, and then He told them to mark the spot.

The reproach of Egypt was the brand of bondage. Israel had been slaves. Their fathers were slaves. The mark of their old master was still on them in the missing sign of the covenant. They walked around for forty years carrying the leftover identity of who they used to be, even though God had already brought them out. Sound familiar?

A lot of you are walking around in 2026 with a brand from a chapter that the Lord already closed. You were divorced and you still introduce yourself by the failure. You were in addiction and you still answer to the addict's name. You were hurt by a church and you still flinch at every pulpit. You were the kid no one picked and you still walk into rooms expecting to be passed over. The Lord delivered you out of it. You are still carrying the reproach of it.

Hear me. The Lord is in the rolling business. The same God who said "today" at Gilgal said "today you will be with me in Paradise" to the thief on the cross (Luke 23:43 ESV). The same God who rolled the shame off Israel rolled the stone off the tomb. He still rolls things away today.

Paul says in Romans 8:1, "There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus" (ESV). Now. Not when you feel worthy. Not when you finally get it together. Now. Isaiah 25:8 promised the reproach of His people would be taken away from all the earth. The cross paid for it. The empty tomb proved it. And the Spirit applies it to your specific shame.

Can I just say this. The reason some of you cannot accept that the reproach is rolled is because you have been picking it back up. The Lord rolled it. You walked back over and grabbed it. You rehearsed the old story one more time in your head this week. You let the enemy whisper the old name and you answered to it. The Lord is not rolling it again because He never said it would stay where you keep putting it. He said today.

Mark the spot. Name the moment. Set a stone in your life that says, this is where the Lord rolled it away and I am not picking it back up.

Tomorrow the Promised Land begins. The manna stops. A Man with a drawn sword shows up. And He says four words that change everything: "Now I have come."

## Application

1. What is the reproach from your old life that you have been carrying long after the Lord brought you out? Name it specifically.
2. Today, set a stone in your life. A written sentence, a date in your journal, a verse on the wall, anything that marks the moment you stopped answering to your old name.

## Challenge

Write out this sentence and put it where you will see it every day this week. "The Lord rolled it away on [today's date]. I am not picking it back up." Then, the next time the old shame voice speaks, read your sentence out loud and walk on.

## Prayer

Father, You said today and I keep saying tomorrow. You rolled it away and I keep walking back to pick it up. Forgive me for living in a chapter You already closed. I have answered to names that died at the cross. I have introduced myself by my reproach when You have already given me a new name. Today, at this Gilgal, I lay it down. Set a stone in my heart so I never forget the day the reproach rolled. In Jesus' name, Amen.
