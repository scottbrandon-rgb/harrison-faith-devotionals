## Devotional

There is an order to this chapter that you do not want to miss. Israel crosses the Jordan. The Lord stops them and says, "Pick up a knife." They circumcise the men. The Lord rolls away the reproach. Then comes verse 10. "While the people of Israel were encamped at Gilgal, they kept the Passover on the fourteenth day of the month in the evening on the plains of Jericho" (ESV). They worship. They remember. They eat the meal of deliverance.

Then verse 11 says they ate the produce of the land. And verse 12 says, "And the manna ceased the day after they ate of the produce of the land." Forty years of bread from heaven, and one morning it just stopped. Because they did not need it anymore.

Hear me. There are some forms of God's provision that only show up when you are willing to leave the wilderness. The manna fed them when there was nothing else. The fruit of Canaan was waiting for them the whole time. They could not eat it until they obeyed their way into it.

And then comes verse 13. Joshua is by Jericho. He lifts up his eyes. A Man is standing there with a drawn sword. Joshua asks the only sensible question, "Are you for us, or for our adversaries?" And the Man answers, "No; but I am the commander of the army of the Lord. Now I have come."

Now I have come.

He did not come at the Jordan. He did not come at the parting. He came after the cut. After the rolling. After the Passover. After the manna stopped. He came in the moment Joshua was finally standing on the other side of his own surrender.

This is the Lord Jesus. We know it is the Lord because Joshua falls on his face and worships, and the Commander receives it. An angel would have refused. We know it is the Lord because He tells Joshua, "Take off your sandals from your feet, for the place where you are standing is holy" (Joshua 5:15 ESV). Same words spoken to Moses at the burning bush in Exodus 3:5. Same Christ. Different mountain. He has come.

Can I just say this. There is a Jericho in your life right now that the Lord is not asking you to figure out. He is asking you to step into the sequence. Surrender what He told you to surrender. Pick up the knife instead of the sword. Let Him cut what you have been defending. Let Him roll away the reproach. Sit down at the table and remember. And then watch what happens when the Commander of the army of the Lord shows up at your wall with a drawn sword and says, "Now I have come."

The wall did not come down because Joshua was clever. The wall came down because the Commander showed up. And the Commander showed up because Joshua walked through the sequence.

So we close the week with the same call we have been circling all along. Name your Jericho. Name the wall you have been trying to bring down by your own strength. Then ask the Lord one question. What is the next obedient step? And take it. He has already gone ahead of you. He is already standing at the wall with a drawn sword. You are not walking to a fight you have to win. You are walking to a Commander who has already come.

## Application

1. What manna are you still asking the Lord to send, when He has already put fruit in front of you and is waiting for you to eat?
2. Name your Jericho. Then write out the one obedient step the Lord is asking you to take this week, and put a date on it.

## Challenge

Take the one step this week. Not a plan to take it. Not a prayer about taking it. Take it. Make the call. Have the conversation. Send the email. Forgive the person. Confess the sin. Cancel the subscription. Whatever the Commander has been telling you, do that thing before Friday ends. He has already come. Now you walk.

## Prayer

Father, I have been asking for manna while You have been offering me the fruit of the land. I have been waiting for You to show up while You have been waiting on me to step into the sequence. Today I surrender. Today I pick up the knife. Today I let You roll it away. Today I sit at Your table and remember. And I trust that when I am finally standing in obedience on the other side of my Jordan, the Commander of the army of the Lord will be there with a drawn sword saying, "Now I have come." Bring down my Jericho Your way. In Jesus' name, Amen.
