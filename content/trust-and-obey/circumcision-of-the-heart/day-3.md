## Devotional

There is a strange detail in Joshua 5 that is easy to skim. Joshua circumcises every male in Israel. Why? Because none of the wilderness generation had been circumcised on the way. For forty years, the children of the rebellion grew up under the language of the covenant but without the sign of it.

They were Israelites by birth. They were not Israelites by mark.

That happens in homes too. A lot of you grew up in a house with a Bible on the coffee table, prayer before meals, church on Sunday, a baptism somewhere in your story. The vocabulary was there. The framework was there. But somewhere along the way you realized you were running on a faith that had been handed to you, never a faith you had ever taken hold of for yourself. You can recite it. You cannot rest on it.

Judges 2:10 is the warning shot. "And all that generation also were gathered to their fathers. And there arose another generation after them who did not know the Lord or the work that he had done for Israel" (ESV). One generation away from forgetting. That is the math. Not three. Not five. One.

Hear me, church. Some of you are wrestling with what your mom and dad handed you. They taught you the rules and never showed you the relationship. They told you to behave and never told you to believe. They gave you a religion to keep instead of a Savior to follow. And now you are sitting in your forties and fifties trying to figure out why the faith you were raised in does not hold weight when life gets hard. It does not hold weight because it was never yours. It was a borrowed coat that never fit.

And some of you are about to do the same thing to your kids.

Can I just say this. Deuteronomy 6:7 told you to teach the Word diligently to your children, talking of it when you sit in your house and when you walk by the way. That is not a Sunday school assignment. That is a kitchen table life. Your kids will not remember the sermons. They will remember whether you prayed in front of them or only complained in front of them. They will remember whether you read the Word at the table or only watched the news at the table. The reproach you have not let God roll away will be the religion you hand them.

There is a better way. Paul reminds Timothy of "a sincere faith that dwelt first in your grandmother Lois and your mother Eunice and now, I am sure, dwells in you as well" (2 Timothy 1:5 ESV). Three generations. Real faith. Cut hearts, not just bowed heads. That is the legacy that lasts.

Today the Lord is asking you to get cut where your father never was. Bring Him the broken inheritance and let Him remake it. Then turn around and hand your kids something better than a system. Hand them the living God.

Tomorrow we go to a place called Gilgal, and we are going to watch the Lord do something only He can do.

## Application

1. What did you inherit from your parents' faith that is still serving you, and what did you inherit that you need to surrender so the Lord can remake it in you?
2. Pick one practice your family does not currently do (praying at the table, reading Scripture aloud, confessing sin to one another, talking about the Lord on the drive home from church) and begin it this week.

## Challenge

Have one conversation today with the person closest to you, whether spouse, child, parent, or close friend, where you tell them one specific way the Lord has met you in the last month. Not a sermon. Not a teaching moment. Just a real account of where the living God showed up in your life. That is how faith gets handed down on purpose instead of by accident.

## Prayer

Father, thank You for the people who told me about You, and forgive me for the places where I have been content with a faith I never owned. I have walked too long with borrowed belief. Today I bring You what I inherited and I bring You what I am passing down. Cut what needs to be cut. Make my faith mine, and make it real enough to leave behind. I do not want to be a generation that quietly forgets You. I do not want my children to wake up one day and not know the work You have done. In Jesus' name, Amen.
