## Devotional

The land is wide open. Jericho is shaking. The kings on the other side of the Jordan have heard what God did to the river, and Joshua 5:1 says their hearts melted. There is no spirit left in them. This is the moment to attack.

And the Lord says, "Stop. Pick up a knife."

He tells Joshua to make flint knives and circumcise every male in Israel. A circumcised man cannot fight. He cannot ride. He cannot run. Go back to Genesis 34 and read what Simeon and Levi did to the men of Shechem after circumcision. Two brothers wiped out a whole city of healing men. That is how vulnerable Israel just made itself.

So here is the picture. Israel is on the doorstep of the Promised Land. Every fighting man is laid low by the Lord's own command. The enemy can see them. The enemy is afraid. And the Lord makes Israel weaker before He lets them stronger.

Hear me. The natural move is to ride the momentum. Win when you are winning. Strike when they are afraid. God moves the other way. The knife comes before the sword. The wound comes before the victory. The vulnerable place comes before the Promised Land.

Paul learned this the hard way. He had a thorn the Lord would not remove, and he asked three times. The answer came back, "My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness" (2 Corinthians 12:9 ESV). Paul did not get the thorn pulled. He got something better. He learned that he was strongest when he had nothing in his hand but the cross.

Listen to me, church. The Lord is going to ask you to put something down today. It will look like the wrong move. It will feel like you are giving up the very thing you need to win. He is asking you to make yourself weak so He can make Himself known. There is something in your life right now that you cannot conquer. You have tried. You have planned. You have gripped it with everything you had. And it is still standing.

The Lord is saying, "Pick up a knife. Let Me cut something out of you first."

What is the thing you keep trying to overcome by sheer force of will? The besetting sin. The same fight with the same person. The financial mess you keep patching. The fear you keep medicating. The pride you keep defending. You attack it with your own sword and it stands right back up. You cannot conquer what God has told you to surrender. You have to lay down on the table. You have to let Him cut.

Tomorrow we will look at how to know what He is cutting. The clue is already in your mouth.

## Application

1. What is the thing in your life you keep trying to conquer by your own willpower, and what would surrender actually cost you?
2. Name one strategy, defense, or comfort you have been using to manage what God has already told you to surrender. Write it down and pray over it by name.

## Challenge

Find ten minutes alone today. No phone. No music. Open a notebook and ask the Lord one question: "What are You asking me to lay down before You give me what is next?" Write down the first thing that comes to mind. Do not edit it. Do not soften it. Bring that piece of paper to your prayer time tomorrow.

## Prayer

Father, I have been trying to win battles You never told me to fight, and I have been losing the ones You already gave me grace for. I am tired. I am stubborn. I want what You promised and I want it on my terms. Today I am picking up the knife instead of the sword. Show me what You want to cut. Make me weak in the places You need to be strong. Take me into the land You promised, and take me there Your way. In Jesus' name, Amen.
