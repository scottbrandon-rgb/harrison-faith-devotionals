## Devotional

Joshua came out to that field armed. Boots on. Standing up. The general, ready to lead.

The first command the Commander gave him was not a battle strategy. It was: take the boots off.

Why the sandals? Listen to this. The sandal is the manufactured thing. It is what a man made and slid in between his own skin and the ground he came from. God says take it off. I want dirt touching dirt. Because Joshua, you came up out of the dust. You are a created thing. You may have placed a sole on your foot, but I am the One who put it in your chest. Don't slide anything man-made between you and the ground I formed you out of.

The same words went to Moses at the burning bush: "Take off your sandals, for the place where you are standing is holy ground." (Exodus 3:5 ESV). Moses the deliverer and Joshua the commander both receive the same command before the biggest moment of their lives. Get your feet bare. Remember who you are. There is the King, and there is the clay. They do not stand on the same plane.

Here is the honest version of that: if you burned a human body all the way down to ash, the raw materials are worth about $4.25. The clothes on your back are worth more. The car in the parking lot is worth more. That is not an insult. It is the truth that frees you from performing. You have value only because God breathed life into the dust and called what He made very good. Romans 9 asks the question plainly: is the clay going to talk back to the Potter?

I need to be honest with you about something I am still learning. When this series started, I told you God was going to change how I preach, prepare, and present. Some weeks I genuinely do not know what He is going to ask next. Dropping Wednesday night discipleship class was one of those steps. I was afraid we would lose momentum, lose people. But success is not defined by what you thought you were building. Success is defined by obedience. And God has not let things drop once.

The boots have to come off. Your plan, your timeline, your definition of what faithfulness looks like. Lay it down. That is where the Commander meets you.

**Worship is not what you bring to God. It is what you lay down before Him.**

## Application
1. What strength or plan are you still leaning on that you have not fully released to God?
2. What is one specific act of obedience this week that feels too costly because of what you might lose if you take it?

## Challenge
Before you do anything for God today, do something to God. Name the thing you are still holding, the plan you have not released, the outcome you are still managing. Then lay it down in prayer. Not a delegation. A surrender. The boots come off first. The battle plan comes after.

## Prayer
Lord, I came in carrying my strength again. I have my version of how this should go and my idea of what faithfulness looks like. I lay it down. I do not want anything manufactured between me and the ground You formed me from. I am clay. You are the Potter. Shape me. What does my lord say to his servant? In Jesus' name, amen.
