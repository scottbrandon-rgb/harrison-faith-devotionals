## Devotional

Look at the name of the man on the ground.

Joshua. In Hebrew, Yehoshua. It means "the LORD saves."

Now look at the name of the One standing over him with a drawn sword. Fifteen hundred years later, an angel is going to appear to a young woman in Nazareth and tell her exactly what to name her baby. The name the angel gives, in the Greek, is Iesous. We translate it Jesus. It is the same name. Both of them are named "the LORD saves."

The author of Hebrews trips on this in chapter 4 verse 8 — the Greek text uses Iesous to refer to Joshua son of Nun, and translators have to note in the margin which Joshua is meant. The names are identical, and the author of Hebrews knows that. He is trading on the typology.

So here is what you are looking at in this field: one Joshua, face down in the dirt. Another Joshua, standing over him with a sword. One led God's people toward the promise. The other is the promise. One walked into Jericho in obedience and won a victory. The other walked to a cross in obedience and became the victory. "He humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." (Philippians 2:8 ESV). The Old Testament has been pointing at Him the whole time.

And here is the thing this passage will not let you forget: the plan of salvation is not the point. The Savior is. Peter says He was "foreknown before the foundation of the world." (1 Peter 1:20 ESV). Before the cross. Before the sin. Before there was a need to die for. There was the Lamb. He preexisted the problem He came to solve.

We tend to treat Jesus like a solution — Someone who shows up when things go wrong. What this passage is saying is that He was there before things went wrong, He will be there after, and right now, in whatever you are walking through, He is not scrambling to respond. He arrived ahead of you.

**He was not there because Joshua needed Him. He was there because He always was.**

## Application
1. Have you been treating Jesus as a solution to your problems rather than the Person your whole life is oriented around? Where does that show up?
2. What is one passage in the Old Testament you could read this week specifically looking for where it points to Christ?

## Challenge
Read Philippians 2:5-11 slowly today, the full Christ hymn. Then go back and read Joshua 5:13-15. Write one sentence about what connects the humility in Philippians to the worship scene in Joshua. The same obedience runs through both texts fifteen hundred years apart. Let that sit on you.

## Prayer
Lord Jesus, You were in this story before it was written. You were Savior before there was a cross. I confess I have been reading the Bible looking for what I can get from it instead of looking for You. You are not the solution I reach for when things break. You are the Person I was made for. Show me who You are in what I am walking through right now. In Jesus' name, amen.
