## Devotional

Yesterday the Commander said, "Now I have come." 
Who is this Commander? 
Why is He so great that Joshua would find himself facedown on the ground? 
Why is this Commander accepting worship? 

As we said yesterday, angels don't receive worship, so this is none other than the Lord Himself. A theophanic appearance of Christ in the OT.  So the one on the ground is called Joshua, and the one standing with a sword drawn is called Joshua.

In Hebrew, the name Joshua means Yehoshua; "the LORD saves." Fifteen hundred years later, an angel is going to appear to a young woman in Nazareth and tell her exactly what to name her baby. The name the angel gives, in the Greek, is Iesous. We translate it Jesus. It is the same name. Both of them are named "the LORD saves."

The author of Hebrews trips on this in chapter 4 verse 8 for the Greek text uses Iesous to refer to Joshua son of Nun, and translators have to note in the margin which Joshua is meant. The names are identical, and the author of Hebrews knows that. He is trading on the typology.

So here is what you are looking at in this field...one Joshua, face down in the dirt and another Joshua, standing over him with a sword. One led God's people toward the promise and the other IS the promise. One walked into Jericho in obedience and won a victory. The other walked to a cross in obedience and became the victory. "He humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." (Philippians 2:8 ESV). The Old Testament has been pointing at Him the whole time.

And here is the thing this passage will not let you forget, the plan of salvation is not the point. The Savior is. Peter says He was "foreknown before the foundation of the world." (1 Peter 1:20 ESV). Before the cross. Before the sin. Before there was a need to die for. There was the Lamb. He preexisted the problem He came to solve.

Here that again...He preexisted the problem He came to solve.

He existed before and He exists after the problem. He is greater than the problem. We tend to treat Jesus like a solution like someone who shows up when things go wrong. What this passage is saying is that He was there before things went wrong, He will be there after, and right now, in whatever you are walking through, He is not scrambling to respond. He arrived ahead of you.

He was not there because Joshua needed Him. He was there because He always was. And before we ever face the problems of Jericho that are waiting for us in the chapter 6 of our lives, we must recognize the person who has revealed Himself in chapter 5.

This is what the scriptures point to; the who is always more important than the what. 

Don't seek the solution, seek the savior.

## Application
1. Have you been treating Jesus as a solution to your problems rather than the Person your whole life is oriented around? Where does that show up?
2. What is one passage in the Old Testament you could read this week specifically looking for where it points to Christ?

## Challenge
Read Philippians 2:5-11 slowly today, the full Christ hymn. Then go back and read Joshua 5:13-15. Write one sentence about what connects the humility in Philippians to the worship scene in Joshua. The same obedience runs through both texts fifteen hundred years apart. Let that sit on you.

## Prayer

Show me who You are in what I am walking through right now. In Jesus' name, amen.
