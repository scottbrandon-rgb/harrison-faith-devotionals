## Devotional

The battle plan for Jericho is not in Joshua chapter 5. It is in chapter 6.

Joshua came out to that field looking for a strategy. What he got was a command to take his boots off and a revelation of who was standing in front of him. No troop movements. No wall diagrams. No blueprint.

The plan comes in the next chapter. After the sandals come off.

God does not hand the blueprint to the man still standing in his own strength. He hands it to the man on his face. And if you have been asking God why He will not show you the plan, that sequence is worth sitting with. You do not get the revelation before the surrender. You get the surrender, and then the revelation comes.

Your knowledge will always be limited until your obedience is not. You want to know the next step, the open door, the path forward. He is not withholding. He is waiting for the boots. The plan only makes sense to a person who has already decided to obey, whatever it turns out to be. You cannot carry chapter 6 instructions with chapter 5 boots still on.

Solomon said it plainly: "Many are the plans in the mind of a man, but it is the purpose of the LORD that will stand." (Proverbs 19:21 ESV). Man attempts plans. God accomplishes purposes. The moment the boots come off, you stop working your list and start coming in line with His.

This week started with the wrong question and it ends with the right one. The right question is not "whose side are You on?" The right question is what Joshua finally asked after he got his face in the dirt: "What does my lord say to his servant?" That question only sounds natural from a person who has already decided they will do it, whatever it is. Jesus put the same tension in one sentence: "Why do you call me 'Lord, Lord,' and not do what I tell you?" (Luke 6:46 ESV). Calling Him Lord with your boots still on is a contradiction. Whose feet are bare?

There are two kinds of people finishing this week. Some of you need to surrender. You have been asking God to get on board with your life, and He is not getting on board. He is asking you to get off the throne. Today is the day. And some of you already know the Lord, but He is optional to you. You negotiate with Him. Remember who He is. Remember who you are. Then watch what He does.

**You don't get chapter 6 until you obey chapter 5.**

## Application
1. Is there an area of your life where you have been waiting for clarity before you obey, when God has already made clear what He wants?
2. What is the one step of obedience that, if you took it this week, would feel like the boots coming off?

## Challenge
Write down one thing you have been waiting to understand before you move. Then ask honestly: is this a knowledge problem or an obedience problem? Most of the time it is the second one. Do not wait for chapter 6. Take one step in chapter 5 today. The plan comes after the surrender.

## Prayer
Lord, I have been asking for chapter 6 while standing in chapter 5 with my boots on. I have made clarity a condition of obedience, and that is backwards. You are the Commander. Your purpose will stand with or without my cooperation. Today I choose cooperation. I lay down my need to understand before I move. What does my lord say to his servant? I will do it. In Jesus' name, amen.
