## Devotional

Have you ever asked someone a simple question and what you got back was an answer that failed to match? Joshua asked a binary question, it was pretty straight forward, "Are you for us or against us?" and "No." is all he got.

Not yes. Not yes for the other side. Just no, wrong question Joshua.

The angel said, "No; but I am the commander of the army of the LORD. Now I have come." What He was telling us was that He did not come to take a side. He came to take command. He is not a soldier waiting to be assigned to a unit, since his sword was not sheathed. No, He is the Commander who arrived with His own orders and His sword already drawn. He has not come to read your plans. He has come to replace them.

Until you understand that, you will keep being confused by God's answers. You ask Him to choose between option A and option B. He answers with C, and you think He is not responding. James 4:3 tells what's happening with our prayers, "You ask and do not receive because you ask wrongly." And yet God in His grace will respond, you're just listening for the wrong thing. He wants to answer the question He is trying to get you to ask, not "Whose side are You on?" but "Who are You?"

That is the only question He always... wants to answer. Not about the plan, the person. The person is what He wants to talk about. When you understand who He is then you realize your plans don't matter. Why? Because Galatians 2:20 tells us why our plans are subject to who He is, "It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me.". See, He does not come alongside your life and add value. He takes over. That is what surrender actually is.

Now watch what Joshua does after the Commander identifies Himself. He falls on his face and worships. And the Commander lets him, that is the tell. Over in Revelation 22, John falls at the feet of an angel and the angel stops him cold, "You must not do that! I am a fellow servant with you." (Revelation 22:9 ESV). Real angels refuse worship. This One takes it without a word of correction. Which means this is not an angel, this is the Lord Himself.

And then He says something worth chewing on, "Now I have come." You have not arrived at any place in your life where you needed God that He was not already there waiting for you. Tomorrow we'll look at why it's important to understand who it is that has come into the picture of our lives. Just remember...

God doesn't come to take sides. He comes to take over.


## Application
1. When God has not answered your prayer the way you expected, what did you assume about Him — that He was against you, indifferent, or somewhere else?
2. What would it look like to stop asking God to bless your next step and instead ask Him to show you who He is in the situation you are already in?

## Challenge
Take your most pressing unanswered prayer. Stop asking God to do the thing. Ask Him to show you who He is in the middle of the waiting. Write that new prayer down. Pray it every day this week and see what shifts.

## Prayer
Lord, I have been asking You to choose between my options instead of asking You to reveal Yourself. I wanted Your power more than Your presence. I wanted a sponsor, not a King. Forgive me. You did not come to take my side. You came to take over my life. That is what I am asking for today. Take over. In Jesus' name, amen.
