## Devotional

You have a wall. You know the one. The marriage that will not bend. The diagnosis that will not move. The child who will not come home. The habit that will not break. You have stared at it so long you have memorized every stone in it.

Israel was standing outside one of those walls. Jericho was a fortress city. Everyone inside had locked the doors and bolted the windows, because the city had heard what the living God did to the Jordan River. No siege towers, no battering rams, no catapults. Just a river of people, a box covered in gold, and a handful of priests with rams' horns.

Then God speaks.

"See, I have given Jericho into your hand, with its king and mighty men of valor." (Joshua 6:2 ESV). Past tense. The gift is already accomplished in God's economy before a single step is taken. Joshua does not yet have Jericho in his hand. The walls are still standing. And God speaks of it as already done.

The battle has already been won. God says so before the march begins. But the victory has not yet been felt. Those are two different things, and the space between them is where most of us are living right now.

What God asks you to do in that space is not panic, not force it, not walk away. Three words are the whole assignment this week. Write them down. Walk. Wait. Worship. Because the shout is His to give, and until He does, those three words are what faithfulness looks like.

The rest of this week unpacks each one. But it all rests on a sentence God said before any of it began: I have given. Past tense. That word came before the march. It was true before the first circuit. It is still true now.

**The battle is already won. The space between God's word and your wall is not doubt. It is the walk.**

## Application
1. What is the wall in front of you that you have stared at so long you have started to wonder if God is going to move?
2. What would it look like today to write God's declaration over your situation — "I have given" — above the problem rather than underneath it?

## Challenge
Write the words "I have given" at the top of a piece of paper. Then write the wall underneath it. Let God's sentence sit above the problem. That is the right order. His word came before the march, and it outranks every stone you have memorized.

## Prayer
Lord, I have a wall, and I have memorized every stone in it. I confess that sometimes I have started to wonder if You are going to move. But You said "I have given" before the march began. I choose to believe it before the walls fall. Teach me to walk, to wait, and to worship until You say shout. In Jesus' name, amen.
