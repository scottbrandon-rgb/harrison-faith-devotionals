## Devotional

The city is "shut up inside and outside because of the people of Israel. None went out, and none came in." (Joshua 6:1 ESV). That is how the narrator opens the scene. Israel on one side. Jericho on the other. Walls up. No way through.

Then God speaks.

"See, I have given Jericho into your hand, with its king and mighty men of valor." (Joshua 6:2 ESV).

Not "I will give." Not "if you march faithfully, I will give." I have given. Past tense. The gift is already accomplished in God's economy before a single step has been taken. Joshua does not yet have Jericho in his hand. The walls are still standing. The armed men inside are still breathing. And God speaks of it as already done.

This is the Hebrew prophetic perfect, a verb form the prophets used to announce future events with the certainty of what has already been finished. God does not announce victories He is considering. He announces victories He has already won. The question is never whether God will do what He said. The question is whether His people will trust the word enough to march toward something they cannot yet see fall.

You are reading a victory report about a war that has not yet been fought. That is what it means to trust the God who speaks in past tense about your future.

Sit with this today: the struggle you are in may already be answered. Not the way you designed it, not on your timeline, but in God's economy it may already be settled. The city was given before the march began. Isaiah put it plainly: "Whoever believes will not be in haste." (Isaiah 28:16 ESV). The one who trusts the word does not panic when the walls are still standing. He marches toward what God has already declared.

You are not waiting on God to act. You are walking toward what He has already done.

**The victory was given before the march began. You are not earning it. You are walking toward it.**

## Application
1. Where have you been treating an unanswered prayer as evidence that God has not yet decided, when He may have already spoken in a language you haven't slowed down enough to hear?
2. What would it look like to take one step today toward something God has spoken over your life, before you see any evidence of it coming?

## Challenge
Write down one thing God has spoken over your life that you have not yet seen. Not as a prayer request. As a received gift. Write it in past tense, the way God spoke to Joshua: "He has given me..." Then take one step today as if it is already true, because according to the One who said it, it is.

## Prayer
Lord, I confess I wait to believe until after I see. But You announce victories before the walls fall. You speak in past tense about what I am still walking toward. Give me the faith that marches toward what You have already given. I will not be in haste. You have spoken. I will walk toward it. In Jesus' name, amen.
