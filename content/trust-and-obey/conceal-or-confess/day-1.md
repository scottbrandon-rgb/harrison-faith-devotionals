## Devotional

I can tell you the exact second a thing stops being something I see and becomes something I want. It is the second I stop walking. The object never changed; my eyes did.

That is where Achan's whole story begins, and he tells us himself. When they finally corner him, he lays out his own confession in order: he saw, he coveted, he took (Joshua 7:21). Three verbs, and the first one is the one we always excuse, the seeing. Surely a man cannot be blamed for what crosses his line of sight.

But seeing and looking are two different things. Seeing happens to you. Looking is a choice you make with your eyes wide open. The plunder of Jericho passed in front of a whole army, and every soldier saw it. Only Achan stopped and looked, and in the looking he made a quiet decision he never said out loud: this belongs to me now.

Here is the part that should sober us. That gold was not loose treasure lying in a street somewhere. It was devoted to the LORD. It already belonged to God before Achan ever laid eyes on it. So the road did not start when he reached into the pile. It started the moment he looked at God's things and called them his options.

Eve walked the same first step. She saw that the tree was good for food and a delight to the eyes (Genesis 3:6), and the seeing slid into looking before her hand ever moved. The serpent did not have to drag her to the fruit. He only had to keep her standing there.

So let me ask you what I keep asking myself. What are you looking at twice? What have you started circling back to, telling yourself a second glance does no harm? Is it harmless, or is it the first stretch of a road you have walked before and know exactly where it ends?

The look is never the finish line. The look is the first step onto the road. Achan did not wake up planning to rob God and bury the evidence under his own floor. He let his eyes rest where they should have kept moving, and the resting did the rest.

Long before the hand grabs, the eye lingers. Long before the sin is done, the heart has already agreed to it in the looking. So guard the looking, because once the eye stops on a thing, the heart starts doing math. It begins to weigh the object, to put a number on what God already owns. And the moment we hang a price tag on something that was never ours to sell, we have taken the second step before we ever felt the first one move us.

## Application

1. What is the one thing your eyes keep returning to that you know you should have walked past by now?
2. The next time that second look comes today, name it out loud as a choice, and turn your eyes somewhere else before the looking starts pricing it.

## Challenge

Pick the one thing you have been looking at twice, the screen, the person, the purchase, the old grudge, and put a guard on it today. Move the app, close the tab, take the long way around, whatever it takes to turn a lingering look back into a passing glance. Do it once, today, before the eye hands the matter over to the heart.

## Prayer

Father, You see what I look at when no one else does, and You saw it before I did. Forgive me for the glances I have let linger, for treating what is Yours as if it were mine to weigh. Give me eyes that keep moving past what I have no business holding, and a heart that stops the road at the first step instead of the last. Guard my looking today, Lord, so that what I see never gets the chance to become what I take. In Jesus name, amen.
