## Devotional

There is a set of scales inside every one of us, and right now, whether we feel the pans move or not, we are weighing something on it.

Achan felt his move. By the time he finally tells the truth, he names the middle step without flinching: he saw, then he coveted (Joshua 7:21). To covet is more than to want. To covet is to hang a price tag on a thing, to decide what it is worth to you, and Achan looked at gold that was devoted to God and decided it was worth more than the command that forbade it.

That is what coveting always does. It runs an appraisal. The eye sets the object on one pan of the scale, and quietly, without announcing it, the heart sets the word of God on the other, and then it watches which side drops. Achan watched the gold drop. He weighed a beautiful cloak from Shinar and 200 shekels of silver and a bar of gold against what God had plainly said, and he ruled that the metal weighed more.

He was not the first to work that scale. Eve set the fruit on one side and God's word on the other, and the fruit won. David stood on a roof, set a woman against the word of God, and the word lost (2 Samuel 11). James names the machinery under all of it: each person is tempted when he is lured and enticed by his own desire (James 1:14). That is the language of a hook with bait on it, and desire is what makes the bait look bigger than the barb.

So slow down with me and look at your own scale. What have you been weighing lately? What is sitting on the pan across from the clear word of God in your life, and which way is it tipping? Be honest about the answer, because the scale tells the truth even when we will not.

Here is the trap we fall into. We tell ourselves we are still deciding. We tell ourselves the wanting is harmless because the hand has not moved yet. But the wanting is the verdict the hand simply has not carried out. Every covetous heart has already ruled in favor of the thing long before the body ever reaches for it.

And that is why coveting is so quiet and so deadly. There is no sound when a heart assigns a higher value to a created thing than to the God who spoke against it. The sin is finished on the inside long before it ever shows on the outside.

So watch the scale today, because the scale always tips before the hand moves. Once your heart decides the gold weighs more than the word, the next step is no longer a question of if. It is only a question of when your hand finally reaches for what your heart has already bought.

## Application

1. What have you quietly decided is worth more than what God has clearly said, even though you have not acted on it yet?
2. Name that one thing today and say the true price out loud: what it will actually cost you and the people standing behind you if the scale keeps tipping the way it is.

## Challenge

Take the one thing you have been weighing against God's word and put it on the scale honestly today. Write down, in one sentence, what you are telling yourself it is worth. Underneath it, write what God has actually said about it. Then look at the two lines side by side and decide, on purpose, which one you are going to obey before the day is over.

## Prayer

Lord, You know the scale inside me better than I do, and You have watched what I keep setting against Your word. Forgive me for the appraisals I have run in secret, for pricing things You never gave me permission to sell. Teach my heart to weigh nothing heavier than what You have said. And when the gold starts to drop in my hand, steady me before the wanting becomes the taking. In Jesus name, amen.
