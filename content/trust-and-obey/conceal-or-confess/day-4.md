## Devotional

The slowest thing about God is the kindest thing about Him, and I have mistaken it for His absence more times than I want to admit or...a lack of care.

When God told Joshua that someone in the camp had sinned, He already knew the name. He could have spoken it from heaven and ended the whole matter in one sentence. Instead He set up a search that crawled, tribe by tribe, then clan by clan, then family by family, then man by man, until the ring finally closed on one person (Joshua 7:16-18).

Slow down and feel how slow that is. God closed the distance one ring at a time, and every single ring was another open door for Achan to stand up and say it himself before the lot ever reached him. The narrowing was not God toying with a trapped man. The narrowing was mercy wearing the clothes of judgment. Peter says it without flinching: the Lord is patient toward you, not wishing that any should perish, but that all should reach repentance (2 Peter 3:9).

And it was bigger than the lots. Walk back through the whole chapter with me. Israel loses at Ai, and Achan says nothing. 36 men are buried, and Achan says nothing. Joshua and the elders lie in the dust and weep, and Achan says nothing. The whole nation is called to consecrate itself, and Achan searches every tent in the camp except his own.

Every one of those was a morning. And the mercy of God is new every morning (Lamentations 3:22-23). The defeat was a morning of mercy. The funerals were a morning of mercy. The weeping, the consecrating, every cast of the lot, morning after morning after morning of mercy held out to a man who would not take it. And Achan spent every one of those mornings holding a shovel instead of lifting his hands.

So hear me, because this is where we fool ourselves. We watch God be patient and we call it God being fine with it. We see the open door and we tell ourselves the door was removed. We notice the floor has not caved in and we decide the secret must be safe. But patience is not permission. A window that keeps narrowing is still a window that closes, and when mercy is refused long enough, the only thing left is the reckoning it was holding back.

Denial does not cancel the reckoning. It only delays it. Achan reached his confession in the end, but he reached it in chains, on the morning the lot finally fell on him and there was nothing left in the ground to hide.

So which morning are you in right now? You are reading this on one of them, with the door still open and the dirt still soft over whatever you put there. The mercy is new today, and you have not used it up. But the rings are getting smaller, and not one of them will keep narrowing forever. Because your sin will be destroyed. The only question still on the table is how.

## Application

1. What patience of God in your life have you quietly been reading as permission?
2. Treat this morning as the mercy it is and confess to God today the one thing you have been waiting out, before the next ring closes.

## Challenge

Stop waiting for the floor to cave in. Take the secret you have been carrying through morning after morning and bring it into the light today, first to God, and then to one person who can help you walk it out. The door is still open this morning. Walk through it before it narrows again.

## Prayer

Lord, You have been patient with me in ways I have taken for granted, and I have called Your mercy permission. Thank You that Your mercies are new every morning, even this one, even after all the mornings I spent holding a shovel. Forgive me for mistaking Your slowness for Your absence. I do not want to be dragged to the truth in chains when the door is open right now. So here it is, today, while there is still time to dig it up. In Jesus name, amen.
