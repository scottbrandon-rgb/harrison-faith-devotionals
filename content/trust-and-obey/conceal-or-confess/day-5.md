## Devotional

Sunday was probably one of, if not the hardest I've ever preached in view of sin. I felt it when I left the building that day and wondered if I had been too strong in my view. I don't think I did because your sin will be destroyed. I need you to understand that before you decide whether it is good news, because everything hangs on the next word, and the next word is not whether but how.

There are only two ways your sin ever comes to an end. There is the destruction of Galatians 2:20, where a man says, I have been crucified with Christ, and it is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me. One of my favs. That is your old self nailed to the cross, dead and buried, while you yourself walk out of the tomb alive. The sin dies and the sinner lives. That is the door I am pleading with you to walk through. Pleading.

Then there is the other destruction, the one Revelation calls the second death, where the sin AND the man who would not let go of it go down into the dark together, still clutching, still hiding, fused to the very thing that killed them. The sin dies either way. There is no third door. 

The only question Achan ever faced, the only question you face, is whether your sin dies without you or with you.

Achan died with his. They took him out to a valley and stoned him, and the place kept the name like a scar that never faded: the Valley of Achor, the valley of trouble (Joshua 7:26). A hidden man, a heap of stones, a whole valley named after one buried secret.

But God was never finished with that valley. Centuries later He walked back to the exact ground where judgment had fallen, and through the prophet Hosea He made a promise no one saw coming: I will make the Valley of Achor a door of hope (Hosea 2:15). The valley of trouble, turned into a doorway out. How can the place of judgment become the place of hope?

Here is how; Achan was one man whose hidden sin spread death to many: 36 soldiers in the dirt at Ai, his own sons and daughters under the stones, a whole camp held guilty over one secret buried under one tent. There was another man exactly like that, the first man, Adam. One man, one act, and death poured out on everyone standing behind him (Romans 5:12).

Then came a second Adam, and He did the opposite of Achan at every turn. Achan hid his sin, and the innocent died for it. Jesus took our sin, the thing we buried in the dark, and carried it out into the daylight where the whole world could watch, up onto a tree, outside the camp (Hebrews 13:12). Achan was guilty and the innocent paid. At the cross, the innocent One paid so the guilty could go free. Achan hid his sin and it buried his family. Jesus lifted our sin into the open, and guilty people walk out the door innocent.

Achan dug a grave, but Jesus turned the grave into a door.

So here is the whole week in one single verse, the same verse Achan only ever lived the first half of, "whoever conceals his transgressions will not prosper, but he who confesses and forsakes them will obtain mercy" (Proverbs 28:13). He concealed, and he did not prosper. The second half of that verse was open to him the entire time, and...it is open to you this morning.

So I am not going to ask whether you have sinned. We both already know the answer. I am going to ask you the one question this whole chapter has been asking from the first page: are you hiding, or are you uncovering? 

What we hide, He uncovers. What we uncover, He hides. 

That is the trade, the grave or the door. And today you get to choose which one your sin walks you into.

## Application

1. Is your sin headed for the kind of death you walk out of alive, or the kind you go down with still holding on?
2. Stop hiding the one thing you have been concealing and confess it to God today, out loud, walking through the door while it is still a door.

## Challenge

Choose the door today. Take the thing you have kept buried, the secret that has been quietly costing you, and bring it into the light by confessing it to God and to one person who will point you back to the cross. You do not have to clean yourself up first. You only have to stop hiding.

## Prayer

Father, my sin is going to be destroyed one way or the other, and today I am choosing the way that lets me walk out alive. Thank You that the innocent One went outside the camp and paid what my hiding earned, that the grave I dug has been turned into a door. I stop hiding right now. I bring it into the light and I give You the truth. Take what I have buried and bury it in Christ, as far as the east is from the west, and let me walk out the door You opened in the valley. In Jesus name, amen.
