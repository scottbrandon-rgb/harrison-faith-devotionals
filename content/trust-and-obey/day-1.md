## Devotional

There is a difference between a God who says "I will give" and a God who says "I have given." One is future tense, the other is finished. And when God spoke to Joshua on the edge of the Promised Land, He did not speak in the language of someday. He spoke in the language of done.

Here's what I see. Joshua is standing at the border of something enormous. Moses is gone. The wilderness is behind him. And God doesn't say "I'm going to work something out for you," He says "I have given to you." Past tense. Completed action. The promise was not pending, it was declared, delivered, and done. The land was already his. The only thing standing between Joshua and the reality of what God had spoken was the ground he had yet to walk on. So the difference between a promise and a present is not whether God has acted, but rather whether we have responded to what He has already done, and that changes how we approach every season of waiting we find ourselves in.

Paul echoes this same reality in Philippians 4:19 when he writes that God will supply every need according to His riches in glory in Christ Jesus. And Peter takes it even further in 2 Peter 1:3 when he says that God's divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness. Not "will grant." Has granted. The promises of God are not IOUs that He's working on fulfilling when the timing is right. They are completed transactions backed by the character of a God who cannot lie.

So let me ask you something. What are you waiting on God to do that He has already done? What promise have you been treating like a distant hope when He has already declared it, already delivered it, already finished it? Because if the promise is already given, then the only question left is whether you and I are willing to put our foot on the ground He's already handed us. That's not a question of God's faithfulness. That's a question of our willingness. And tomorrow, we're going to look at what happens when we stop walking before the promise is fully possessed.

## Application

1. What promise from God have you been treating as "someday" that He may have already declared as "done"?
2. Is there an area of your life where you are waiting for God to act when He may be waiting for you to step?

## Challenge

Today, identify one promise from Scripture that speaks directly to a need in your life right now. Write it down, read it out loud, and ask God to show you where your foot needs to land.

## Prayer

Father, I confess that I have treated Your promises like distant possibilities when You have already spoken them into completion. Forgive me for standing at the edge of what You have given and calling it uncertain. Give me the faith to believe that what You have declared is already done, and give me the courage to take the first step onto the ground You have already placed under my feet. I trust You today, not because the path is clear, but because You are faithful. In Jesus' name, amen.
