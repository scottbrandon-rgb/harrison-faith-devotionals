## Devotional

God told Joshua to be strong and very courageous, and then immediately told him why. Not because the battles ahead would be easy, but because the obedience ahead would be costly. "Be careful to do according to all the law." Not some of it. Not the parts that feel natural. All of it. The strength God was calling for was not the strength to fight enemies, it was the strength to keep walking in obedience when everything in you wants to sit down.

Here's what I need you to know. Trusting and obeying are not the same thing. Trusting is the belief it takes that leads to action. Obeying is the acting out of that belief. You can trust God and still not obey Him, because trust without movement is just agreement. James 2:17 says that faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead. So faith is not the finish line, it is the fuel. And obedience is the engine that faith was designed to power.

And sometimes obedience does not look like letting go. Sometimes surrender is not the releasing of your ways, sometimes it's letting go of the excuses that prevent you from starting something, trying something, risking something. Maybe God is not asking you to stop a behavior right now. Maybe He is asking you to start one. Maybe the obedience He requires is not about what you need to lay down but about what you need to pick up, and the excuses you've been carrying are heavier than the assignment He is trying to hand you. Hebrews 12:1 says to lay aside every weight and the sin which clings so closely. Sometimes the weight is not a sin at all, it is a mindset, a fear, a comfortable rhythm that has convinced you it is protecting you when really it is preventing you from running the race God has set before you.

Phew. That one may sting a little. But it needs to, because the cost of the next step is not always pain. Sometimes the cost is simply the willingness to stop making excuses and move. And God does not ask you to move without telling you why. He says "that you may have good success wherever you go." The obedience is not pointless. It leads somewhere. It produces something. And when tomorrow comes, we are going to look at the power that meets you on the other side of that step.

## Application

1. Is there something God has asked you to start that you have been making excuses not to begin?
2. What is the difference right now between what you trust God for and what you are actually obeying Him in?

## Challenge

Today, name one thing God has been asking you to start. Not stop. Start. Write it down, set a date to begin, and tell one person about it so that your obedience has accountability.

## Prayer

Father, I confess that I have confused agreeing with You for obeying You. I have trusted You in my mind but not with my feet. I have carried excuses longer than I have carried Your assignments, and I have called that wisdom when it was really just fear. Give me the strength and the courage You gave Joshua, not to fight a battle, but to take the next step of obedience that You have already laid out in front of me. I want my faith to have works. I want my trust to lead to action. Help me begin today. In Jesus' name, amen.
