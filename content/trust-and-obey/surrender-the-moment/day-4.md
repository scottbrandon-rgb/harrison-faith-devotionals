## Devotional

God makes a statement in Joshua 1:5 that should stop every one of us in our tracks. "No man shall be able to stand before you all the days of your life." Not some days. Not the good days. All the days. And then He backs it up with something even bigger. "Just as I was with Moses, so I will be with you. I will not leave you or forsake you." The promise is not just power, it is presence, and the two are inseparable.

So what I'm seeing is this. The power to take new ground does not come from your ability to fight harder. It comes from whose presence you are standing in. Romans 8:31 asks the question plainly, "If God is for us, who can be against us?" And Paul doesn't leave it theoretical. He lists every possible opposition, tribulation, distress, persecution, famine, danger, sword, and then in verse 37 he says that in all these things we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us. Not just conquerors. More than conquerors. The original word there is "hyper-nike," which means super victory. You didn't just win the match, you dominated it through Christ. And 1 John 4:4 tells us why, because He who is in you is greater than he who is in the world. We have conquering power because we dwell in constant presence.

Think of it like this. If you have ever stood on the shore and watched a surfer harness the force of a massive wave, you have seen a picture of what happens when your will comes into alignment with something far greater than yourself. When the surfer surrenders to the direction of the wave, nothing can stand against him. It is the same with us. When we surrender our will to God's will, there is a power that moves through our lives that no enemy, no circumstance, no opposition can deny. Now, that does not mean the water is always calm or that the ride is always smooth. Alignment with God's will does not guarantee the absence of suffering, but it guarantees His presence in the middle of it, and that presence is where the power lives.

This is incredible, because it means the power you need for the next step of obedience is not something you have to manufacture. It is something you step into. It is already there. God is not asking you to generate the strength to obey Him. He is asking you to walk close enough to Him that His strength becomes yours. And tomorrow, we are going to look at what it means to walk in that presence, not just for a moment, but for the long haul.

## Application

1. Do you tend to approach spiritual battles as something you must fight in your own strength, or are you drawing from the presence of God?
2. Where in your life do you need to be reminded that God's presence is not just a comfort but a source of conquering power?

## Challenge

Today, read Romans 8:31 through 39 slowly, out loud if possible. As you read each verse, ask yourself whether you are living as though these words are actually true for your life right now, not someday, but today.

## Prayer

God, I confess that I have tried to fight battles in my own strength and wondered why I felt defeated. I have forgotten that Your presence is not just a comfort for the weary, it is power for the obedient. Remind me today that the same God who was with Moses and with Joshua is with me, and that no opposition in my life is bigger than the One who dwells in me. I surrender my will to Yours, not because the path is easy, but because Your presence on that path makes me more than a conqueror. In Jesus' name, amen.
