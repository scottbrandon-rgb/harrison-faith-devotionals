## Devotional

The story of Rahab does not end in Joshua 2. It does not even end when the walls of Jericho fall and her household is brought out safely while the city burns around her. The story of Rahab ends in a genealogy. And that genealogy changes the way you read everything that came before it.

Matthew 1:5. Salmon the father of Boaz by Rahab. She married an Israelite. The Gentile prostitute from a condemned city, the woman who hid two spies under flax on her roof and tied a scarlet cord in her window, married into the people of God. And her son Boaz married Ruth, another Gentile woman, a widow from Moab who had nothing going for her except loyalty and a God she chose to call her own. And Ruth became the mother of Obed. And Obed the father of Jesse. And Jesse the father of David. And David, through many generations, became the forefather of Jesus Christ.

Think about that slowly. The woman who hung the scarlet cord becomes one of the mothers in the bloodline of the One whose blood would become the scarlet cord for every nation on earth.

And this was not the first time God used something scarlet to mark his people for preservation while judgment fell on everything outside of it. In Egypt, the night of the Passover, every house marked by the blood of a lamb on the doorpost was spared while the firstborn of every unmarked house died. The blood was the sign. The blood was the tiqvah, the hope and the rope, stretched across the top of the door. And in the fullness of time, on a hill outside Jerusalem, one more scarlet cord went up. This one not made of thread or of lamb's blood but of the blood of the Son of God, hung on a cross, Romans 10:9 says, "If you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." That is the cord. That is the tiqvah. That is the hope and the rope hanging in the window of the universe for anyone who will look up and tie themselves to it.

Now hear me on this last thing, because this is where the text stops being a war story and becomes a gospel story. Rahab was not simply rescued. She was redeemed. There is a difference. Rescued means you survive what was going to kill you. Redeemed means you are brought inside the family of the one who saved you and given a name in his story. Rahab did not escape Jericho as a survivor. She was brought into Israel as a daughter, and through Israel into the line of the Messiah, the One whose blood would mark the windows of the world. The woman who acted on a promise before the promise was fulfilled did not just get out alive. She became part of the story that was leading to the rescue of everyone else.

Rahab's faith did not save her on the day Jericho fell. Rahab's faith saved her the day she tied the cord. The salvation became visible on the day of the rescue, but it was settled the night she stretched that scarlet thread out her window while the city was still standing. The cord is not what saves you. Christ saves you. But the cord is what proves to your own soul that you actually believe he does.

So tie something this week. The marriage. The kid. The body. The bondage. The bank account. The grief. Pick the area and pick the cord. Make the call. Have the conversation. Walk into the room. Hang it before the wall falls. A faith that stands up, stands out, before the promise ever stands fulfilled.

## Application

1. Rahab was not just rescued, she was brought into the family line that led to Jesus. How does that change the way you see your own story of faith, not just what you are saved from but what you are being brought into?

2. Romans 10:9 is the scarlet cord hanging in the window of the universe. Is there someone in your life who needs to see it? What would tying that cord on their behalf look like for you this week?

## Challenge

Write down the name of one person in your life who does not yet know Christ. Pray for them by name today. Then take one visible step toward them this week. Invite them to the table. Make the call you have been putting off. Reconcile the relationship that has been keeping the gospel out of reach. Hang the cord where they can see it.

## Prayer

Father, Rahab was not just rescued. She was redeemed. She was brought into the family and given a name in the line of the One who would save the world. That is the kind of story you write for people who tie the cord before the proof arrives. I am asking you to write that story with my life. Not just to rescue me but to redeem me. Not just to save me but to use me. And Lord, the cord you hung on that cross has been there for two thousand years. Help me hang it where someone I love can see it this week. In Jesus' name, amen.
