## Devotional

Catch the timing of Joshua 2:21. She tied the cord before she saw the army. She tied it before the trumpets sounded. She tied it before a single Israelite shouted. She tied it before one brick of Jericho's wall ever cracked. The promise had not been fulfilled, the deliverance had not come, and Joshua was not even camped on the right side of the Jordan yet.

Rahab acted on the word of two strangers she had just met, who served a God she had only heard about, on the strength of a rescue that had not happened yet, in a city that still looked, from every possible angle, like it was going to win.

That is faith. Not faith after the proof. Faith before the proof. Faith on the front side of the miracle.

Hebrews 11:1 says faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen. Not the celebration of things already proven. Not the relief of things that already came through. The conviction, the settled internal confidence, of things not seen yet. And Hebrews 11:31 makes sure we do not miss what that looked like for Rahab specifically. "By faith Rahab the prostitute did not perish with those who were disobedient, because she had given a friendly welcome to the spies." She makes the Hall of Faith. The Gentile prostitute from a condemned city with walls the world had never seen the equal of, she is in the Hall of Faith, not because she watched the walls fall but because she tied the cord before they did.

Here is where most of us live our faith life differently than Rahab did. We are waiting for the wall to fall before we tie the cord. We are waiting for the marriage to feel different before we start treating our spouse the way Scripture demands. We are waiting for the body to be healed before we start praising the Healer. We are waiting for the finances to turn around before we start giving. We are waiting for the proof before we put anything visible in the window. And in the meantime, our hope stays invisible, and invisible hope looks no different from the hope of the unbeliever.

But Rahab understood something the king of Jericho and every citizen in that city never figured out. You do not tie after the rescue. You tie before it. You act on the word before the word becomes visible. You hang the hope before the promise lands. Because that is what faith is.

So I want to ask you a direct question this morning. Where are you waiting for the wall to fall before you tie the cord? What appointment have you been putting off until you feel ready? What conversation have you been avoiding until the other person shows signs of changing first? What step of obedience have you been holding back until you get more clarity? Rahab did not have clarity. She had a word from two strangers and a window and a cord. And she stretched the cord out the window in a city that was still standing and still threatening and still looked like it was going to win.

Faith that stays internal stays invisible. And invisible faith does not look any different from a window with nothing in it at all. Tomorrow, we find out where that scarlet cord was always pointing.

## Application

1. Where are you waiting for proof before you act on what you believe God is going to do? What is one step you could take today on the front side of that miracle?

2. Hebrews 11 puts Rahab in the Hall of Faith specifically because of what she did before the walls fell. What would it mean for your faith to be described the same way? What is the action that is still waiting to be taken?

## Challenge

Identify one area of your life where you are waiting for circumstances to change before you act on what you say you believe. Today, before the wall falls, take one step. Not after you feel ready, not after you have more confirmation, not after the other person goes first. Act on the word you already have. Tie the cord while the city is still standing.

## Prayer

Lord, I want to be a person who ties the cord before the wall falls. I do not want the kind of faith that only believes when the evidence is already in front of me. You are pleased by faith on the front side of the miracle, and I am asking you to grow that in me. Today I am putting my hope in the window before the rescue comes. I am trusting you before I can see you working. Hold me to that. In Jesus' name, amen.
