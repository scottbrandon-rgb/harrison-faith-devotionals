## Devotional

The testimony of God demands a response. It always has. It always will. The question is never whether you will respond to what you hear about God. The question is what your response will be.

Everyone in Jericho has heard the same report. The Red Sea. Sihon and Og, the kings east of the Jordan, completely destroyed. The testimony of God's power has reached every house in that city. And in Joshua 2, we get three responses. Watch them carefully, because one of them is yours.

The king responded with control. The moment he hears that two Israelite spies have entered Jericho, he does not wait. He sends men to Rahab's door and sets up a counter-operation to neutralize the threat. He is going to handle this himself. Hear me, church. That is a more familiar response than most of us want to admit. When the silence of God stretches longer than we can comfortably stand, the temptation is not always to give up. Sometimes the temptation is to take over. "I've prayed long enough. I know what needs to happen. I'll make it happen myself." That is not unbelief wearing a mask. That is fear wearing a strategy. Fear with a plan is still fear.

The citizens responded differently. Verse 11, Rahab tells the spies, "there was no spirit left in any man because of you." The Hebrew word there is ruach, and it means breath. The whole city had the wind knocked out of it. You know that feeling. It is the moment the doctor says the word you did not want to hear. It is the moment the marriage you thought you had walks out the door. It is the moment the prodigal calls and you find out where they actually are. The breath goes. The courage goes. You can see what is coming and your feet will not move.

And then there is Rahab. Same testimony. Same data. Same impending judgment coming toward the same city walls. And she does not collapse and she does not grab the wheel. She confesses. Verse 9, "I know that the Lord has given you the land." Verse 11, "the Lord your God, he is God in the heavens above and on the earth beneath." She is not pleading. She is praising. She then asks the spies to show her hesed, the covenantal loyal love of God, the kind of love God described to Moses in Exodus 34 as steadfast and promise-bound. Not contractual love, which says if you perform then I will respond. Hesed says because I am who I am, I will. Rahab knows that a covenant people carry the character of their covenant God. So she invokes the covenant, not the circumstances.

Here is what stops me every time I read this. Rahab did not have better information than the king did. She did not have a clearer view or a safer position inside that city. She had the exact same testimony everyone else had. And she did something different with it. Because a faith that stands up in confidence will always stand out in evidence.

The king grabbed the wheel. The citizens lost their breath. Ask yourself honestly today which one of those looks most familiar right now. Because Rahab had a third option, and she took it, and tomorrow we are going to look at exactly what she did with what she believed.

## Application

1. Which of the three responses to God's testimony is most recognizable in your life right now: reaching for control, losing your breath, or confessing what you actually know to be true? What is driving that response?

2. Rahab asked for hesed, the covenant love of God, even before she had any proof it would be extended to her. What would it look like for you to invoke God's covenant character today instead of negotiating based on your circumstances?

## Challenge

Today, write down one thing you actually believe about God, in plain language, the way Rahab said it out loud to two strangers. Not what you are hoping. Not what you are asking for. What you actually know about who he is. Read it back to yourself. That is a confession. That is where a faith that stands up begins.

## Prayer

Lord, I confess that I have grabbed the wheel more times than I want to count. I have lost my breath more times than I want to admit. But today I am choosing to confess what I actually believe about you, out loud, even before the proof arrives. You are God in the heavens above and on the earth beneath. You are the God of steadfast, covenant, loyal love. I know it. Help me stop acting like I only suspect it. In Jesus' name, amen.
