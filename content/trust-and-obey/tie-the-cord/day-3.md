## Devotional

There is a Hebrew word buried in Joshua 2 that changes the way you read Rahab's story. The word is tiqvah. In verses 18 and 21, the spies tell Rahab to tie a scarlet cord in her window so that when Israel comes to take the city, the soldiers will know which house to pass over. That cord is tiqvah. It is rope. It is the physical, visible, tangible thread she hangs in the window.

But here is what the English does not show you. Every other place tiqvah appears in the Old Testament, it does not mean rope. It means hope. It comes from the root word qavah, which means to wait, to look expectantly, to lean forward toward something that is coming. You hear it in Jeremiah 29:11, where God says he knows the plans he has for you, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope. That word, hope, is tiqvah. Job used it in chapter 14 when he said even a tree that is cut down still has tiqvah, still has hope, because the stump may yet sprout again. Isaiah 40:31 uses the same root when it says those who wait on the Lord will renew their strength.

So when Rahab ties the cord in her window, the Hebrew is doing something the English translation cannot carry on its own. She is not just tying a rope. She is tying a hope. She is letting her expectation of deliverance become physical and visible. Her faith has a color. It has a knot. It has a window. Her hope has left the inside of her chest and found the outside of her house where anyone passing by can see it.

That is what your faith is supposed to do.

James 2 puts the question plainly. "What good is it, my brothers, if someone says he has faith but does not have works? Can that faith save him? So also faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead." James is not teaching salvation by works. James is saying that faith without visible expression is not yet faith. It is sentiment. It is feeling. It is a request with no cord in the window. A window with no tiqvah hanging in it looks exactly like the window of someone who never believed in God at all. Which brings us back to the question that opened this week. The reason our hope in God looks identical to the hope of the unbeliever is not because God is not real. It is because hope that never moves is hope that never shows.

So let me ask you where your cord is. Not where your prayer is. Not where your feeling lives inside your chest. Where is the visible expression of what you believe? What does your hope for your marriage actually look like in the way you treat your spouse today? What does your hope for the prodigal look like beyond the prayer you already prayed twelve years ago? What does your hope for healing look like in what you have actually done with the unforgiveness you have been carrying? What does your hope for financial provision look like in whether you have started giving instead of grasping? What does your hope for freedom look like in whether you have made the call you keep saying you will make?

Rahab tied the cord before anyone came to rescue her. She did not tie it on the day the walls fell. She tied it the night the spies left. The promise was not yet fulfilled, the army was not yet at the gate, and she stretched that scarlet thread out her window anyway. That timing matters more than we realize. Tomorrow, we look at it directly.

## Application

1. James says faith without works is not yet faith. In one area where you have been praying and hoping, what visible action have you taken that makes your hope physical? If the answer is nothing, what is stopping you?

2. The cord in Rahab's window was visible to anyone who looked at her house. If someone looked at the window of your life in the area you are most urgently trusting God right now, would they see anything there?

## Challenge

Pick one area where you have been praying and hoping but have not yet taken visible action. Today, take one step that makes your hope physical. Make the call. Schedule the appointment. Confess the sin out loud to someone who can pray for you. Write the check. Have the conversation you have been avoiding. Whatever the cord is for your particular window, tie it today, not after you have more clarity.

## Prayer

Father, I have kept too much of my hope inside my chest. I have prayed and called it faith. I have felt and called it trust. Today I am asking you to make my hope visible. I am taking one step toward what I say I believe, and I am asking you to meet me in that step. Let my tiqvah show. Let the window of my life actually say something about what I believe about you. In Jesus' name, amen.
