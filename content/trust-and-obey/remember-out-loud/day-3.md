## Devotional

Before you decide what to do with your testimony stone, you need to see what it is actually carrying. Because the testimony stone is not just your story. It is pointing at something far bigger than your story, and once you see what it is pointing at, leaving it in the water becomes a completely different kind of decision.

Stand back from the Jordan crossing and look at the wider frame.

Verse 10: the priests stood firm in the riverbed holding the ark. They bore the presence of God on their shoulders. They stood in the place where the judgment of the water should fall so the people could pass through on dry ground. That is what a mediator does. Hebrews 7:24-25 says we have a better mediator than those priests, one who always lives to intercede for the people He leads through.

Verse 11: the ark goes first into the riverbed and last out of it. It waits until every person is across before it leaves. The forerunner. Hebrews 6:20 calls Jesus the forerunner who has gone on our behalf into the presence of God.

Verse 13: forty thousand came up out of the water armed and commissioned for conquest. Centuries later, another Joshua walked down into that same Jordan River. Came up out of that same water. The heavens tore open. The Spirit descended. The voice of the Father spoke. And He went into the wilderness to begin His own conquest. Same river. Same trajectory. Matthew 3:13-17.

Now listen to what the writer of Hebrews does with this. Hebrews 4:8 says it plainly: if Joshua had given them rest, God would not have spoken of another day later on. The writer of Hebrews is not being subtle. He is telling you straight that the first Joshua is a shadow and the greater Joshua is the substance. The name Joshua in Hebrew is Yehoshua. In Greek, Yehoshua becomes Iesous. In English, Iesous is the name we say as Jesus. Same name. Greater scale.

Verses 15-17: the priests came up out of the Jordan. That is the language of burial and resurrection. Romans 6:4 picks up this pattern and runs with it. We were buried with Him through baptism into death, and raised to walk in newness of life. Baptism is a stone pile. It is a physical, repeated, public act designed to keep the same story from being forgotten by every generation that follows.

Verse 18: the waters came back. Watch this carefully, because most people miss it. The Jordan crossing did not abolish death. It held death back for the duration of the crossing, and then the river returned exactly the way it always had been. The people were safely across. But death was still death on the other side. The priests stood in the water and held it. But it came back.

Until the greater Joshua showed up.

The priests stood in the water. Christ went under it. The priests held death back. Christ swallowed it up. First Corinthians 15:54 says that at the resurrection, death will be swallowed up in victory. That is not the Jordan. That is the cross and the empty tomb, and it makes the Jordan crossing a signpost pointing at something that dwarfs it entirely.

This is what the testimony stone is carrying. Your story is not the monument. The exchange is the monument. Your story is the evidence that the exchange happened in you personally. Which means when you leave the testimony stone in the water, you are not just keeping your experience to yourself. You are leaving the monument to the exchange buried. Tomorrow that exchange gets named in both directions, because it goes further than most of us have let it go.

## Application

1. How does seeing the Jordan crossing as a signpost pointing to Christ change the weight you give your own testimony?
2. Where do you see the pattern of "burial and resurrection" in your own story, the specific place where God went down with you so you could come up?

## Challenge

Read Romans 6:1-11 today in full. As you read, write down one sentence that names where your own life connects to the burial and resurrection pattern Paul describes. That sentence is a stone. It belongs on the bank, not in the river.

## Prayer

Father, I confess I have thought of my testimony as my story. Today I see it was always Your story written on my life. The priests at the Jordan, the greater Joshua in the same water, the empty tomb on the other side of death, all of it was pointing to what You were doing from before I was born. Make me a monument to the exchange. Let the pile I leave behind point people past me and toward You. In Jesus' name, Amen.
