## Week Overview

This week in Stones of Remembrance we are working through Joshua 4 and asking one question: what does God require of you the day after He moves? The answer is a construction project. Twelve stones. Twelve men. Shoulder the weight and stack it somewhere visible. Each day this week builds the case that remembrance is not a feeling, it is a structure. Biblical memory has parts: a stone pile, a child's question, and a parent who answers out loud. We are going to look at what those stones are actually carrying, the typological weight of the Jordan crossing, the exchange that happened when Christ went under the water in our place, and what it means that He commissioned you to proclaim what He could not proclaim from a sealed tomb. By Friday you will have one concrete assignment: pull one stone out of the river this week and put it somewhere your people can ask about it.

Daily titles:

Day 2 - Stones Don't Remember Themselves
Day 3 - What the Pile Is Carrying
