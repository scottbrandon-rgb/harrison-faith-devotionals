## Devotional

Here is something I want you to sit with for a second. The Lord did not wait until Joshua had a strategy before He gave him a promise. He gave the promise first. Way before the Jordan crossing. Way before Jericho. Way before any of the cities and the battles and the decisions Joshua would have to make. God spoke the promise over the problem before the problem even had a name.

That is not just a Joshua thing. That is the pattern all the way through Scripture, and once you see it you cannot unsee it.

Go back to Genesis 3. The moment sin entered the world, before God pronounced consequence on Adam, He turned to the serpent and gave a promise. Genesis 3:15. He said the seed of the woman would bruise the head of the serpent. That was the first messianic prophecy in the Bible. God had already spoken the solution before He spoke the sentence. Long before the cross, long before the manger, long before any of it, the plan was already in place.

Look at Genesis 12. God called Abraham into covenant before Egypt. Before the cries of slavery. Before the wilderness. Before Babylon. Every single time Israel found itself at the bottom, there was already a promise that preceded the fall. That is not coincidence. That is the character of God.

Romans 5:8 says while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. Not after we got our act together. Not after we cleaned up the mess. While. The provision was already made before we knew we needed it. Revelation 13:8 tells us the Lamb was slain before the foundation of the world. Your name was written in that book before the first problem you ever had. Think about what that means. Your worst day, God already had a plan for it before time began.

Here is what I need you to hear today. You are not behind on what God has for you. You are not in a situation He did not see coming. The problem you are praying about right now, the one that feels like it came out of nowhere, the Lord looked at it long before you did. And He has already made provision for it.

So quit telling God you need something He has already given you. I know that sounds direct. But please hear my heart on this. We pray like God is late, like He missed the urgency of the situation, like we have to bring Him up to speed. And the whole time He is saying, if you would just walk in what I have already spoken over your life.

Second Peter 1:3 says His divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness. All things. Not most things. Not the easy things. All of it. What you are asking God to give you, He has already placed inside the promise. The question is not whether the provision is there. The question is whether you will walk in it.

Joshua had a promise before he had a plan. And tomorrow we are going to look at what God required of Joshua in order to walk in that promise without compromise.

## Application

1. Can you identify a specific promise from Scripture that speaks directly to the weight you are carrying right now? If not, what would it look like to go looking for one?
2. How does it change the way you pray when you realize God already made provision for your problem before you knew you had it?

## Challenge

Find one promise in Scripture that speaks to the heaviest thing you are carrying this week. Write it on a notecard or in your phone. Read it out loud three times today. Not as a formula, but as a declaration that the promise is already ahead of you.

## Prayer

Father, forgive me for praying like You are behind. Forgive me for acting like I have to remind You of what I need. Today I am choosing to believe that You already saw this coming, and You already made a way through it. Help me walk in what You have already promised instead of waiting for something new. Your word is enough. You are enough. I trust You with what I cannot see and what I cannot carry. In Jesus' name, amen.
