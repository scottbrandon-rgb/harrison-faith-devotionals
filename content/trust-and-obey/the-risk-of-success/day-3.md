## Devotional

"Do not turn from it to the right hand or to the left." That is deviation language. It is the language of a road with coordinates so specific that God has already ruled out two directions before you ever take the first step. Right and left are not options to pray about, but rather the two paths God has already marked as wrong.

Here's what I see. Joshua is standing at the edge of the Promised Land with a divine scouting report he did not have to request. God has already told him where to go. The whole book of Joshua becomes the story of a man who refuses to turn right or left. Jericho is the first test, and the strategy God gives him reads more like a worship liturgy than a siege plan. March around the city once a day for six days. On the seventh day march around it seven times. Blow trumpets. Shout. Watch the walls fall. Every military commander in Joshua's ranks is thinking the same thing. This is going to get us killed.

Joshua obeys anyway. Why? Because the promise in verse 5 is louder than the plan in verse 8. Can I say this plainly. Jericho is obedience before failure. It is obedience before the outcome is proven. It is obedience when the strategy looks like foolishness and the only justification for following it is that God said so. That is the category most of us resist living in, because we want God to show us the payoff before we take the step. But faith that waits for the payoff before it obeys is not faith. It is negotiation. Hebrews 11:8 says of Abraham that "by faith Abraham obeyed when he was called to go out to a place that he was to receive as an inheritance. And he went out, not knowing where he was going." The obedience came before the geography.

Hear my heart on this. Partial obedience is disobedience in a respectable suit. Turning right or left is not a minor course correction, it is the slow abandonment of the path. You do not start off running the wrong direction. You start off angling a few degrees off the line God drew, and then a few more, and then one day you look up and the tower of Jericho is nowhere in sight because you are miles from the coordinates God gave you. The right and left of Joshua 1:7 are the respectable versions of rebellion. They are the compromises that never quite feel like disobedience but end up there anyway. Tomorrow we walk into the harder category, the one that comes after the failure, because there is a town called Ai that most of us know more intimately than we want to admit.

## Application

1. Where have you been angling a few degrees off the line God drew, telling yourself it is not really disobedience because it has not taken you far yet?
2. What is the Jericho in front of you right now, the thing that looks like foolishness from the outside but you know God is telling you to do?

## Challenge

Today, take a hard look at your calendar, your spending, and your conversations from the last two weeks. For each one ask the same question. Is this a right turn, a left turn, or is this staying on the road God marked? Then correct course on one specific thing before you go to bed.

## Prayer

Father, I confess I have called partial obedience wisdom when it has really been disobedience in a respectable suit. I have turned a few degrees off the path and told myself I was still facing forward. Forgive me. Bring me back to the line You drew. Give me the strength to obey before I see the outcome, and the trust to believe Your plan is better than my strategy, even when Your plan looks like a worship liturgy instead of a siege. In Jesus' name, amen.
