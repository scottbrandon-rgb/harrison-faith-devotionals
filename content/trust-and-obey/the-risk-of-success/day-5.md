## Devotional

"Have I not commanded you?" That is God's final pastoral word to Joshua before Joshua takes the first step into the Jordan. Listen carefully to the question. It is not a rebuke. It is not "why are you still standing there?" It is "have I not already said what I am about to say again, because I know you need to hear it one more time?" God comes back to Joshua before Joshua moves, because God knows what the first step feels like. And then God says the same words He said in verse 5 but with two new pieces. "Be strong and courageous. Do not be frightened. Do not be dismayed." That is the command on the front. "For the Lord your God is with you wherever you go." That is the presence on the back. The command and the presence are welded together in the same breath. You cannot separate the strength God asks for from the presence God provides.

Here's what I need you to know. The presence of God is not the reward for your obedience, but rather the ground beneath it. He does not go with you after you prove yourself. He goes with you so you can move at all. If you are standing on this side of your Jordan waiting to feel God before you obey, you will be waiting a long time, because the presence is not the proof, it is the platform. Paul says it this way in Philippians 2:13, "It is God who works in you, both to will and to work for His good pleasure." The willing is His work, and so is the working. You are not manufacturing courage from nothing, you are responding to a presence that is already there.

And so the settled confidence of verse 9 is not the absence of fear. It is the presence of God displacing fear as the loudest voice in the room. Joshua is going to feel fear at Jericho. Joshua is going to feel shame at Ai. Joshua is going to feel the weight at every single one of the 31 kings he defeats in the land. The promise is not that he will stop feeling those things, but rather that those things will no longer be louder than the voice of the God walking beside him.

Church, this is what multiplies in a life. You cannot multiply in others what you have not modeled in yourself. Joshua leads Israel across the Jordan, through the walls, back up the hill of their defeat, into covenant renewal at Shechem, and all the way to the end of his life saying "as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord." And the people follow. They follow because he went first. The God who is telling you to go is also the God who is going with you. Arise. Go over this Jordan. The Lord your God is with you wherever you go.

## Application

1. Where have you been waiting to feel God's presence before you obey, instead of obeying because He has already said He is with you?
2. Who is watching how you walk right now, and what are you modeling for them about trust, obedience, and the nearness of God?

## Challenge

Today, take the step. Not the step you have been praying about for months, but the step God has already told you to take. His presence is not the reward at the top of the hill. It is the ground beneath your feet this morning. Move.

## Prayer

Father, thank You for going before me and walking beside me. Forgive me for waiting to feel You before I would obey You. Your presence is not my reward, it is my ground. Make Your voice louder than my fear. Give me the steady confidence that comes not from the absence of doubt but from Your nearness. And by my obedience let others see what it looks like to walk with You. In Jesus' name, amen.
