## Devotional

When I was a kid, my uncle Randy took us out barefoot skiing. We had two ropes. A short rope and a long rope. The short rope was for control. You could cut left, cut right, do just about anything you wanted on it. The long rope was different. You just kind of floated out there, easy, smooth, no fight in it.

Here is the thing we kept doing wrong. When we started, we would hold both ropes at the same time. My uncle would gun it and you would come up out of the water, and if your board was pointed the wrong direction, your arms were about to find out exactly how strong they were. I can tell you, I was too stubborn to let go. I was sixteen years old. There were girls watching. I was not going to come up out of that water looking like I had no idea what I was doing.

But at some point, when everything was going sideways and I was losing control, I had to make a decision. Drop the short rope. Snap back to the long one. And it hurts when you catch it. It really does snap you. But once you were there, it was the smoothest ride you had ever taken.

Here is what I want you to see. You and I are doing the same thing. We love the short rope because we love control. The world makes more sense when we are the ones running it. There is order, there are expectations, there are clear boundaries. So we grip the short rope until our knuckles go white and tell ourselves that is just good planning, good stewardship, good leadership.

Joshua is holding both ropes when we meet him in chapter one. Moses is dead. Nobody asked Joshua if he was ready for this. Nobody took a vote. The greatest leader in Israel's history was gone, and every eye in the camp turned toward Joshua. Two million people with two million questions. What are we eating? Where are we going? What is the plan?

Joshua did not ask for this. It was just handed to him. And that matters, because a lot of what you are carrying right now is the same way. A spouse walked out. A parent died. A company closed. A diagnosis came back. You did not go looking for any of it, and now you are out there holding both ropes, trying to look like you have it figured out while the boat is already running.

The weight is certain. That part is not up for debate. What is not certain is the outcome. And that is exactly where Joshua is standing. He cannot guarantee what happens next. All he knows is that Moses is gone, the people are watching, and the pressure of it all is already pressing down on every part of him.

Here is what I need you to know today. The weight does not disqualify you from trusting God. It qualifies you for it. Because the Lord never waited for the pressure to ease before He spoke to Joshua. He spoke right into it. And what He said to Joshua, He is saying to you right now. Arise. Go. Not when the weight lifts. Now.

Tomorrow we are going to look at something that changes the whole picture. You were never sent into the weight without something already waiting for you on the other side of it.

## Application

1. What responsibility were you given that you never asked for? How have you been carrying it, in your own strength or in God's?
2. Where in your life are you still holding both ropes, trying to maintain control while asking God to step in? What is keeping you from letting go of the short one?

## Challenge

Name one thing in your life today that you have been gripping with both hands, something God has been asking you to release. Write it down on paper. Do not try to solve it yet. Just name it before Him and tell Him it is too heavy for you to carry alone.

## Prayer

Father, I want to be honest with You. I like the short rope. I like knowing what is coming. I like having my hands on things I can manage. But today I am naming the weight that was handed to me, and I am asking You to help me trust You with it. You did not wait for Joshua to get it together before You spoke. You spoke right into the middle of it. Speak into mine. Give me the faith today to release what I have been gripping and to hold on to what You have already promised. In Jesus' name, amen.
