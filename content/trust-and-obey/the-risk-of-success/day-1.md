## Devotional

There is a sentence at the top of Joshua 1 that people read right past, and it is the most terrifying sentence in the entire book. "Moses my servant is dead." With those words, forty years of buffer between a man and the weight of leading two million people is gone. The one who carried the staff, parted the sea, and climbed the mountain... dead. And God's next instruction to Joshua is not a transition sermon, not a grief retreat, not a sabbatical. God's next words are "arise, go over this Jordan."

Hear me. There is a moment in every life when the person who carried the weight for you is gone. For some of you it was a parent. For others it was a pastor, a mentor, a boss, a grandmother who prayed for you every morning of your life. Whatever name you gave them, they were the buffer between you and the weight, and now the buffer is gone. The weight is on your shoulders. And God is not offering to carry it back, but rather telling you to go.

Here's what I need you to know. The fear underneath that weight is rarely the fear of failing itself. It is the fear of what failure would mean. If I fail, does the promise fail with me? If I stumble, does God's word stumble too? If I get this wrong, was I the one thing standing between God's word and its fulfillment? That spiral is the tax we pay for believing our performance determines the promise. Here is the truth Joshua had to learn. God does not promote out of desperation. He promotes because His word needs a vessel, and what Moses had carried had to be passed to the next man. The Greek word the New Testament uses for this kind of handing down is paradidomi... to deliver over, to entrust. It is the same word Paul uses in 1 Corinthians 15:3 when he writes, "I delivered to you as of first importance what I also received." The weight was never meant to die with the last generation. It was always meant to be handed forward.

So hear this carefully. The weight on your shoulders this morning is not a punishment for being faithful, but rather the evidence of faithfulness handed forward. Someone saw faithfulness in you and passed you what they could no longer carry. The pressure is not proof God forgot you. The pressure is proof you're next. Tomorrow we look at what God does before He ever tells Joshua to move, because grace always shows up before the assignment does.

## Application

1. What weight has come to rest on your shoulders recently that someone else used to carry, and are you treating it as a punishment or as an assignment?
2. Where in your life have you been waiting for someone else to step back in and carry what God has actually handed to you?

## Challenge

Today, name the weight out loud. Not in generalities, but specifically. Tell God what has landed on your shoulders, where you have been tempted to treat it as a punishment, and what it would look like to pick it up as an assignment instead. Then take one step forward in it before the day ends.

## Prayer

Father, I confess I have been treating the weight on my shoulders as though You had forgotten me. I have complained about the pressure instead of seeing the trust behind it. Help me see what You see. If this weight is the evidence that You have handed me what someone else could no longer carry, give me the courage to pick it up with both hands. I am not next by accident. I am next because You said so. In Jesus' name, amen.
