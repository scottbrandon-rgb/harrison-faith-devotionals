## Devotional

There is a verse in this chapter I used to read fast, the way you hurry past something you would rather not look at. A man is hanged on a tree. At sundown his body is cut down, thrown at the city gate, and buried under a pile of rocks (Joshua 8:29). It sits in the middle of a victory chapter like a bloodstain on a celebration.

Why is it here? It is here because of an old law. Deuteronomy said that a man hanged on a tree is under the curse of God, and his body must come down before nightfall so the land is not defiled (Deuteronomy 21:22 and 23). Joshua keeps that law to the letter. The body is down by sunset. To everyone watching, one word hung in the air over that tree: cursed.

Now watch this, because the geography is about to preach. Right after the cursed king is buried, Joshua climbs Mount Ebal, the mountain of cursing, and builds an altar (Joshua 8:30). He offers a sacrifice for sin in the exact place the curse is pronounced. On the hill where you would expect only condemnation, blood is poured out and peace is made. The curse and the cure stand on the same mountain.

Centuries later Paul looks back at that old law about the tree and tells us where it had been pointing the whole time. "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us, for it is written, 'Cursed is everyone who is hanged on a tree'" (Galatians 3:13). The king of Ai dying cursed at the gate was a shadow. The substance came later, outside another city's gate, on another tree, where the only innocent man who ever lived took the curse that belonged to the guilty.

He took the curse you earned. He took it into His own body, the way the altar on Ebal swallowed the judgment that should have fallen on Israel. He took it all the way down into a borrowed grave and walked out leaving it behind. Your guilt was real, and it was carried in full by Another.

This is the word the condemned heart cannot hear often enough. If you have spent your life standing on Ebal, bracing for the curse to fall, look again at the tree. The curse already fell. It fell on Him. The punishment that hung over your name landed on a hill outside Jerusalem two thousand years ago, and there is nothing left of it for you to dread.

So the choice in the valley is no longer a gamble. The curse has a place to go, and it has already gone there. Tomorrow we step back into the valley one last time, where the word is read to every soul in earshot, and a whole nation is told to choose.

## Application

1. What verdict over your life are you still carrying that Christ already carried to the tree?
2. Say that verdict out loud to God by name, and thank Him for taking it in His own body.

## Challenge

Write down the one verdict over your life you are most afraid is true. Then write the word carried across it, and underneath it write Galatians 3:13. Keep the paper somewhere you will see it this week.

## Prayer

Lord Jesus, I have stood on the mountain of cursing too long, bracing for a judgment that already fell on You. Thank You for becoming a curse for me, for carrying to the tree the verdict that had my name on it. I lay down the guilt I keep picking back up. Teach my heart to believe that what You carried, I no longer have to. Help me live as one whose curse is gone. In Your name, amen.
