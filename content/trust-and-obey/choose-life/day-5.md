## Devotional

We have walked eleven weeks through the book of Joshua together. We crossed a flooded Jordan on dry ground. We marched around Jericho until the walls came down. We stood in the wreckage Achan's hidden sin left behind. It all comes down to this, to a whole nation standing in a valley between two mountains, listening to one question they are not allowed to dodge.

When Joshua finished reading the law that day, the writer makes a point of telling us exactly who was listening. "There was not a word of all that Moses commanded that Joshua did not read before all the assembly of Israel, and the women, and the little ones, and the sojourners who lived among them" (Joshua 8:35). He names every group. The men who fought and the women who held the camp together, the children too young to follow half of it, even the sojourner, the outsider who had thrown in his lot with Israel the way Rahab once did. Nobody in that valley was too small to be included, and nobody was too far outside to belong.

Then comes the choice, the one Moses had set before them on this very ground: "I have set before you life and death, blessing and curse. Therefore choose life, that you and your offspring may live" (Deuteronomy 30:19). Read those last three words again. Your offspring. The choice you make in the valley does not stay in the valley. It echoes down the hillside into the lives of the children standing behind you, listening to every amen you shout.

This is what we forget when we shrink faith down to a private deal between us and God. You belong to a whole. The day you choose life, you are choosing it for your house, for your road, for the dark pockets up in these hills where the light has not reached yet. There are homes in Harrison that need it. There are schools that need it. There are rooms you walk into every week where you are the only light that ever shows up.

Go back to the very first blessing God ever spoke over a human being: be fruitful, multiply, fill the earth, take dominion (Genesis 1:28). Then hear Jesus pick up that same blessing on a mountain of His own. "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Go therefore and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:18 and 19). Now the conquest moves by light instead of by the sword. You cannot carry that light to the nations if you will not first carry it across your own kitchen and down your own street.

For eleven weeks one phrase has held this whole series together: trust and obey. We have learned the hard way that you will not obey a God you do not trust, so every failure to obey is really a failure to believe He is who He says He is. He is the God of the flooded Jordan. He is the God of the fallen wall. He is the God who took your curse on a tree. A God like that can be trusted, and a God who can be trusted can be obeyed.

So choose life. Choose it for yourself. Choose it for your house. Choose it for the hills where God planted you, because the One who set the choice in front of you already walked down into the valley first and chose you.

## Application

1. Whose life, beside your own, is being shaped by the choice you keep putting off?
2. Choose one person in your house or on your street and carry the light to them this week with a specific, visible act.

## Challenge

Name the one place God has planted you, your house, your job, your street. Pick one person there and do one concrete thing today that carries the light of Christ to them, something they can actually see.

## Prayer

Father, I have made my faith too small and too private. You have set life and death in front of me, and I choose life, for myself, for my house, and for the place where You have planted me. Make me light in the dark pockets where You have put me. Give me the courage to carry You across my own kitchen and down my own street before I ever talk about the nations. I trust You, so I will obey You. In Jesus' name, amen.
