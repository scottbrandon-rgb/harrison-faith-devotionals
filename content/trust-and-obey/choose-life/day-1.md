## Devotional

I have failed at the same thing twice and walked away sure that God had run out of words for me. That is the lie I believe on the far side of a defeat. The silence feels permanent. The door I slammed feels bolted from the other side.

Israel knew that silence. They had just been routed at a small town called Ai, a name that means the ruin, and the ruin had ruined them (Joshua 7). One man, Achan, had hidden stolen treasure under his tent, and his secret sin cost the whole camp the battle. Before that first failed assault, God had said nothing. No command, no promise, just a nation marching on its own confidence. Then the sin was dealt with. Into the quiet the voice comes back: "Do not fear and do not be dismayed... See, I have given into your hand the king of Ai" (Joshua 8:1).

Read that slowly. Given. Past tense. He had handed them the win before a single sword left its sheath, given it before they marched, given it before they earned one inch of it. A father does not hand his son the keys after the boy proves he can drive. He hands them over first, and then teaches him to drive. The trust comes first, and the trust is what makes the lesson possible.

Now look at what God lets them keep. At Jericho, every piece of plunder was devoted to God, off limits, untouchable, and the man who grabbed it died for it. At Ai, God says, "Only its spoil and its livestock you shall take as plunder for yourselves" (Joshua 8:2). The very thing that killed Achan is now placed in the open hands of the whole nation as a gift. Grace does that. Grace takes the thing you once stole in the dark and sets it on the table in the light.

Here is what I need you to know. Your battle was never just yours. Joshua's fight was the next link in a promise God made to Abraham generations before (Genesis 12:2), a promise rolling toward a cross and an empty tomb. You are not a soldier swinging away in a field nobody can see. You belong to a whole. Your obedience strengthens the body, and your hiding wounds it.

So the question on the morning after your worst defeat is not whether God is finished with you. He is already speaking. The real question is whether you will trust the voice that calls the ground yours before you ever set foot on it.

Tomorrow we watch what Joshua does with a win that big. You would expect him to charge the next city. Instead he stops, and he builds something, and he remembers.

## Application

1. Where has an old failure convinced you that God has gone silent on you for good?
2. Name one good thing you have been holding at arm's length out of guilt, and receive it today as a gift instead of a reward.

## Challenge

Write down the one defeat you are sure disqualified you. Beside it, write out Joshua 8:1 in your own words, as God saying it to you this morning, and read it back out loud before you do anything else today.

## Prayer

Father, I have believed the lie that my failures sent You into silence. Thank You that You speak again, that You handed me the victory before I ever earned it, and that You call me back into the fight as one You have already forgiven. Teach me to receive what You give instead of straining to deserve it. Remind me that my life is bound up with Your people and Your promise, and that nothing I do for You is too small to matter. In Jesus' name, amen.
