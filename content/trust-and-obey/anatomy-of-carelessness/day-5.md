## Devotional

The camp could not move. Israel had lost at Ai, Joshua was on his face, and the Lord told him exactly why the army was stuck. "There are devoted things in your midst, O Israel. You cannot stand before your enemies until you take away the devoted things from among you" (Joshua 7:13). Somewhere in that camp was a tent, and under the floor of that tent was a hole, and in that hole was everything Achan had taken and hidden. Until it came up, the whole nation stood still.

That is how a buried thing works. It does not stay private. It stops the march. There is a win you have been quietly taking credit for. There is an Ai you keep insisting you can handle alone. There is a prayer you have meant to pray for weeks and never have. The camp of your life does not move forward until the thing in the floor comes up into the light.

So let me ask the question this whole week has been driving toward. Are you careless? Have you let the success of your life turn faithful obedience into proud confidence? Have you grown so sure from past wins that prayer feels unnecessary? When understanding turns into frustration, and prayer turns into complaint, carelessness has already set in.

Here is the good news buried inside this hard chapter. Achan took what was not his and hid it where no one could see. Jesus did the opposite, out in the open. He took what He did not owe, the full weight of our sin, and He hid none of it. He carried it where the whole world could watch, up a hill and onto a cross, because God made Him to be sin who knew no sin (2 Corinthians 5:21). What Achan buried brought death to the camp. What Jesus carried into the light brought life to it.

That is why confession is not the threat we treat it as. Solomon told us, whoever conceals his transgressions will not prosper, but he who confesses and forsakes them will obtain mercy (Proverbs 28:13). What stays covered cannot be cleansed. What you drag into the light can be. If we walk in the light, as He is in the light, the blood of Jesus His Son cleanses us from all sin (1 John 1:7).

Remember where this all started. Back at Jericho the orders were simple, walk and wait and worship, and obedience brought the wall down. Then came that one small word, but, and carelessness brought the army to a halt. The whole difference between the two is what you do with the thing in your tent.

So stop hiding it. Dig it up. Bring it into the open where God can finally deal with it. The only thing you carry to this is surrender, the same way the only thing Israel ever carried to the battle was obedience. Quit handling Ai alone. Get on your face before the next battle, not after the funeral of your own plan. Then watch the camp start to move again.

## Application

1. What is the devoted thing buried in the floor of your tent right now, the win, the Ai, or the prayer you have been hiding?
2. Confess that one buried thing today, out loud to God, and if it involves someone else, to a trusted person who can walk with you.

## Challenge

Name the one thing you have kept covered and bring it into the light today. Tell God plainly what it is, and take the first concrete step to forsake it before the day ends.

## Prayer

Father, I have buried things in the floor of my life and wondered why the camp would not move. Forgive me for hiding what only You can heal. Thank You that Jesus carried my sin into the open and onto the cross, hiding none of it, so that I would never have to. I bring You my surrender today, the way Israel was only ever meant to bring obedience. Pull the covered things into Your light and cleanse me. Get me on my face before the next battle, and move me forward again. In Jesus' name, amen.
