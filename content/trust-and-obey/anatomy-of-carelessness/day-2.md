## Devotional

There was a summer I asked God what we should do on Wednesday nights, and I could not even find teachers for the classes. His whole answer was one word. Pray. Just pray. So I argued with Him. I said, Lord, if all we do is pray, we will lose the momentum we spent years building. He answered with something I have not been able to shake since. It is a good thing it was not you who built that momentum. Because whatever you build in your own strength, you will have to maintain in your own strength.

That is the trap hiding inside a victory. Watch Israel walk straight into it. They had just taken Jericho, the largest fortified city in the whole conquest, and here is the scouting report they bring home. "Do not make the whole people toil up there, for they are few" (Joshua 7:3). Stay on that last line. For they are few. The same men who watched God flatten a city with marching and trumpets are now sizing up a smaller town and deciding they have this one handled.

Great obedience had produced a great victory. That great victory had quietly produced great confidence. This is exactly where we have to pay attention, because the morning after the win is when we stand most exposed. Pastor Burton said it to us plainly. The valleys of seduction always follow the pinnacles of success.

How fast Israel forgot that Jericho was never theirs to begin with. They did not earn it. God handed it to them, and He chose a strange way to do it so no one could mistake whose victory it was. The orders were nothing but march, wait, and shout. The only thing Israel carried to that wall was obedience, and everything else came down from heaven.

We forget the same way. It would be foolish for me to look at this church growing and credit our programs, our personalities, our preaching. The growth is the power of God, nothing smaller. The moment I think more about myself than I think about God, pride is already standing in the room. Israel was great because God was greater, and when that order flips, we are in trouble.

Hear me, because this is the strange math of grace. The blessing on a church can ruin a church if she forgets where the blessing came from. The blessing on a life can ruin a life the very same way. That is why the Lord keeps the requirement simple. He asks us to do justice, to love kindness, to walk humbly with our God (Micah 6:8). Solomon left us the warning we keep stepping over. Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall (Proverbs 16:18).

So carelessness puts on pride first. Pride never stays single, though. It has a child, and that child already has a plan drawn up. Tomorrow we meet it, the moment we start writing our own orders.

## Application

1. Where has a recent victory quietly turned into confidence in yourself? What win are you still standing on as if you built it?
2. Pick one thing you have been maintaining in your own strength. Hand it back to God in prayer today and ask Him to carry it.

## Challenge

Today, find the most recent success you are proud of and say out loud who actually built it. Then thank God for it by name before you ask Him for a single new thing.

## Prayer

Lord, I confess that I am most in danger right after You have been good to me. I take the victory You handed me and start to wear it like something I earned. Forgive my pride. Remind me that whatever I build in my own strength I will have to hold up in my own strength, and I am not strong enough for that. I was great only because You were greater. Keep me low. Keep me grateful. Keep me leaning on You the morning after the win. In Jesus' name, amen.
