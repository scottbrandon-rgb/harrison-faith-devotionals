## Devotional
A but always shrinks whatever came before it. I love you, but. I am healthy, but. You feel the floor tilt, do you not? So watch what gets smaller here. "But the people of Israel broke faith in regard to the devoted things" (Joshua 7:1). The walls of Jericho had just fallen. The largest fortified city in the whole conquest was theirs. The very next stroke of the writer's pen is a negation.

That phrase, broke faith, is a single Hebrew word, ma'al. It carries the idea of treachery, of trespassing against something holy, of taking what was set apart for God and treating it as common. Achan took some of the devoted things and buried them in the floor of his tent (Joshua 7:21). A private sin, hidden in the dirt where it could not be seen, or so he thought.

Here is the part that bothered me. Read the Lord's words again. "Israel has sinned" (Joshua 7:11). One man did the stealing, and the verdict fell on the whole nation.

My honest first reaction was, that's not fair. Then I felt like Cain standing over his brother, asking the Lord, am I my brother's keeper (Genesis 4:9). So I closed my mouth and asked Him to teach me. Here is where He started. All through Scripture you find holy men flat on their faces, Moses and Nehemiah and Daniel and Isaiah, begging God to forgive the sins of their people, sins that were never even their own.

Think about Sodom. God was on His way to judge it, and He paused to ask whether a few righteous people might stay His hand (Genesis 18:32). If a handful of the righteous could hold back the judgment of God, that tells us something notable. Righteousness stays the hand of God and its absence invites it in. Peter said...Judgment begins at the household of God (1 Peter 4:17).

So let me ask you what He asked me. If the land is dark, whose job is the light? Can darkness make itself bright? No. That is the lamp's work. Then is it not our work to shine over this nation, and the work of this church to shine over this city? Those already in the dark are already judged. God holds the light accountable for whether it shined.

Now you see why we cannot do this alone. None of us burns bright enough or far enough by ourselves. We have to gather our light with others, or we sin the very way Israel sinned. Their corporate sin was carelessness and Achan's sin was covetousness.

Carelessness has an anatomy. Three marks, and each one fathers the next. Pride. Presumption. Prayerlessness. Tomorrow we start where it starts, with pride on the morning after the victory of Jericho.

## Application

1. If one man's hidden sin could lose a battle for a whole nation, what might my hidden carelessness be costing the people around me?
2. Name one place where you have stopped gathering your light with others. What is one step today to reconnect it to the body?

## Challenge

Today, before you do anything else, name the last victory in your life and finish this sentence: God supplied this, and all I brought was obedience. State it plainly and let it reshape the obedience of the day.

## Prayer

Father, I have read past small words and missed the warnings hidden inside them. Show me the buts in my own life, the places where my faithfulness gave way to carelessness. Make me a lamp that shines and not a tent that hides. Teach me to carry the weight of my people the way Your holy men carried theirs, on my face, asking for mercy. I bring You nothing but obedience this morning, and I trust You to supply the rest. In Jesus' name, amen.
