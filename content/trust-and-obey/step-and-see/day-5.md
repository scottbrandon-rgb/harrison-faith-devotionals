## Devotional

Verse seventeen tells you what happened after the river thinned. "The priests bearing the ark of the covenant of the LORD stood firmly on dry ground in the midst of the Jordan, and all Israel was passing over on dry ground until all the nation had finished passing over the Jordan."

The priests stood in the middle of the river. Not at the edge where they could see the dry bank and feel the pull of it. In the middle. And they stood there while the entire nation crossed. That was the job. Stay in the water until the last person is through.

There are some of you who stepped into the river weeks ago. Months ago. You did what God told you to do. You are carrying the weight. You are holding the word up. You are praying, believing, obeying. And you see no change. The circumstance is the same. The water is still moving against you. And somewhere in the back of your mind you are calculating how long you have to do this before you are allowed to step out.

Stay in the water.

Keep praying for that lost loved one, because God is at Adam. Keep praying for your marriage, because God is at Adam. Keep praying for your health. Keep praying for your finances, your family, that situation at work that will not resolve. God is at Adam. The word has gone out. The work is underway. Do not step out of the river so soon that you remove the very thing God is using to hold back the flood for someone else who still needs to cross.

The enemy is going to name your staying stubbornness. He will tell you it looks like you are not trusting God to move, like you are holding onto something He already said to release. Staying in the water is faithfulness. The enemy is wrong, and you know whose voice that is.

Now hear me on this, because I cannot close this week without it. The most powerful foreshadowing in this entire chapter is something Joshua could see but could not yet name. The priests stepped into the waters, and a way was prepared for all the nation to cross on dry ground. There was another Priest coming. One who would step into the ultimate flood stage, the waters of death themselves, and when His feet hit that water, He would prepare a crossing that no one could ever take from you. His name is Jesus, and He has already made the way.

If you are in the water right now, you are not in it alone. You are following a Priest who has already walked where you are walking. And He stood in that river until the last person crossed.

Go back through every Jordan in Scott Brandon's life, and I promise you this: not one of those significant crossing moments was ever instantaneous. There was a whole lot of waiting. But there was also a whole lot of faithfulness. And the Lord who was faithful in every one of those crossings is the same Lord who is at Adam right now.

God really is among you.

## Application

1. Is there a prayer, a promise, or an act of obedience you have been tempted to abandon because the wait has stretched longer than you expected? What would it look like to stay one more day?
2. The priests stood in the river until the last person crossed. Who else might be depending on your faithfulness in this season, and how does that expand your understanding of why God is asking you to hold your position?

## Challenge

Write down the name of the person or situation you have been interceding for the longest. Write beside it: "God is at Adam." Put it somewhere you will see it every morning this week. That is your post. Stay in it.

## Prayer

Father, some days the water is still moving fast and I cannot see any change, and I am tempted to step out and call it done. Steady my feet. Keep me in the river. Let the record of what You have done become the anchor for what I am trusting You to do. And Lord, let me be that kind of faithful, the kind that stays in the water until the last person crosses. Use my obedience for something bigger than what I can see from where I am standing. You are the living God. I will trust that today. In Jesus' name, amen.
