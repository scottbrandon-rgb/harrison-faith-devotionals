## Devotional

The command God gives in verse thirteen is specific enough to make you uncomfortable. He does not say "I will dry up the river and then you will walk across." He says when the soles of the priests' feet rest in the water, the waters will be cut off from flowing. The sequence matters. Wet feet first. Dry ground second.

We want it the other way. Every one of us does. Show me the evidence and then I will obey. Show me the outcome before I put anything at risk. Dry up the river first and I will get in it. But that's now how God does it, does He? The power is in the river. If you want to see what He is going to do, you have to be obedient to what He has already said.

And Israel had reason to expect it differently. They had grown up with the story of the Red Sea. Moses lifted his staff and the waters moved before a single sandal touched the water. Boom, instantaneous! That was in their bones and in their fireside stories. And here at the Jordan, the order is different. Here you step into the water and then it parts. God changed the terms and He reserves the right to do that.

Here's what I need you to know. The evidence for God's power is settled. The question on the table right now is whether you are obedient to what He has already told you to do. Because His power is in the river, and it has been there all along. The bank is where you keep options open, where the wilderness is still a soft retreat behind you, where fear stays comfortable enough to live in. Step off the bank and the only thing available is faith.

I said Sunday that there are things in your life you are supposed to be doing and you are not doing them yet. You have been thinking about it. Going around and around in your head. Anxiety has crept up because somewhere underneath all of that thinking, you know there are things the Lord has already put in your heart. Things to stop. Things to start. Things to engage. And your prayer has been "Lord, why aren't You moving?" He says, "I am in the Jordan. But your feet are not."

Think about what it took for those priests to walk into a flood stage current carrying the ark of the covenant. The current was fast. The water was cold. And God's instruction was not to cross it. It was to stand still inside it. Hold the position. Let the water press against you and do not move until I tell you to. Feel the turmoil of the situation and know you're not alone in it.

That is an act of obedience that requires a level of trust most of us have not been asked for yet. Some of you have been asked for it. You know the thing He told you to do. You are fighting your own voice about it. Talking yourself out of it because the river is too fast and the water is too cold and the crossing looks too impassible, no, it's impossible. The Lord is saying, "Step in." That is where He meets you. If you want to be where the Lord is, join Him, in the river.

Tomorrow you are going to find out why the wait inside the river is not the absence of God's word. It is the fulfillment of it.

## Application

1. What specific thing has God told you to do that you have been waiting for better conditions before you obey? What is the honest reason your feet are not in the water yet?
2. Where in your past has God asked you to obey before you could see the outcome? What did stepping in teach you that standing on the bank never would have?

## Challenge

Name the one step you have been standing on the bank about. Today, take it. Not the whole crossing. The first step. Write down what that step is before you go to bed tonight, and write beside it what you are trusting God to do when your feet hit the water.

## Prayer

Father, You know where my feet are and You know they are not in the water yet. I have been asking You to move before I obey, and I see now that is fear wearing a theological argument. Forgive me. I want to step in. I am afraid. Move my feet toward the thing You have already put in my heart to do, and meet me in the river when I do. You said Your power is there. I am choosing to believe it today. In Jesus' name, amen.
