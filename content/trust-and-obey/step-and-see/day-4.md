## Devotional

When the priests stepped into the Jordan, the water did not part at Jericho. It parted at a city called Adam, 15.6 miles upstream. The last documentation of the Jordan's natural flood stage current, recorded in the 1850s before modern industry began diverting the river, put the water moving at ten miles per hour. At 15 miles, that means once God shut off the water at Adam, it took at least an hour and a half for the river in front of the priests to begin thinning.

An hour and a half. Standing in cold, fast water. Holding the ark. Watching nothing happen.

Israel had the Red Sea story in their bones. In that story, Moses lifted his staff and the waters parted instantly. They were expecting the same thing here. And they are standing in a flood stage current and the river is not moving. The priests are looking at Joshua. Joshua is looking upstream. I want you to feel the weight of that hour and a half, because that is probably where you are right now.

You stepped in. You obeyed. You started doing what God told you to do. And nothing has changed yet. The circumstance is the same. The flood stage is the same. The enemy is telling you the wait is an answer. He is telling you that God did not hear, or that you did not pray right, or that your faith is too small. You know who you are talking to when you hear that. He is the one who, when he speaks, lies naturally. That is his nature and that is his strategy, and he does his best work right here in the hour and a half.

Go to Daniel chapter 10, verse 12. Daniel has been fasting and praying for three weeks and receiving nothing. Then the angel Gabriel shows up and says, "Fear not, Daniel, for from the first day that you set your heart to understand and humbled yourself before your God, your words have been heard, and I have come because of your words." The first day. Flip back to Daniel 9, verse 23. "At the beginning of your pleas for mercy a word went out."

At the beginning. God heard Daniel from the moment he opened his mouth. Three weeks of silence was not three weeks of divine indifference. There was a battle happening in the unseen realm and the word was already on its way. It was just 15.6 miles upstream.

Hear me. The moment those priests stepped into the Jordan, God was at Adam. The word had already left His mouth. The water was already being stopped. The priests could not see it, could not feel it, could not know it. But the work was underway from the first step.

Your prayer has been heard. From the first day. From the beginning of your plea. God is at Adam right now. The water is shutting off upstream. You just cannot see it yet.

Stay in the river. What you do with that tomorrow may be the most important decision of this whole week.

## Application

1. What prayer have you been tempted to abandon because the answer seems delayed? How does Daniel 10:12, "from the first day your words have been heard," change how you see that wait?
2. The gap between God acting at Adam and Israel seeing the river thin was at least an hour and a half. Where have you been measuring God's faithfulness by what you can see downstream rather than trusting what He is doing upstream?

## Challenge

Find one promise God has spoken to you through Scripture that directly addresses what you are waiting on. Write it out and date it. Underneath it write this: "From the first day, my words have been heard." Carry that into the wait today.

## Prayer

Father, I confess that I have read the wait as silence and silence as absence. Forgive me for that. Your word says from the beginning of my plea You heard me, and I am going to stand on that today. I cannot see what is happening at Adam. I do not need to. You are there. The work is underway. Help me stop measuring Your faithfulness by what I can see downstream, and hold my feet steady in the water until the river thins. In Jesus' name, amen.
