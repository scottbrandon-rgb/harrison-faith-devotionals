## Devotional

One of the remarkable qualities of salt is its permanence. Pure salt doesn’t corrode. It doesn’t spoil. It doesn’t lose its nature with age or exposure. In the ancient world, this quality made it a powerful symbol of lasting covenant.

That’s the image Jesus calls to mind in Matthew 5:13. If you are in Christ, your identity is secure. The covenant you stand in is unshakable. No persecution, no cultural decay, no shifting trends can dissolve who you are in Him.

But here’s the caution—while salt’s nature doesn’t change, its effectiveness can be compromised. We can’t lose the identity of being salt, but we can lose the impact of living as salt. That’s why Jesus warns about contamination and mixture.

If…then. If God has made me part of an indestructible covenant, then I should live with the confidence and stability that comes from that promise. My environment may be unstable, but my calling is not.

The world needs to see steady believers—disciples who endure, who hold their ground, who stay faithful even when everything else is shifting. That’s the kind of salt that points to the faithfulness of God.

## Application

1. How does knowing you’re in an unshakable covenant give you courage in daily life? What pressures or distractions tempt you to compromise your effectiveness as salt? How can you model stability and endurance in your current situation?

## Challenge

Write down one truth about God’s covenant faithfulness and keep it in view today as a reminder that your identity in Him is indestructible.

## Prayer

Lord, thank You for making me part of a covenant that cannot be broken. Help me to live with the steadiness and faithfulness that reflects Your unchanging nature. Keep me from compromise, and let my life point to Your enduring promises. Amen.
