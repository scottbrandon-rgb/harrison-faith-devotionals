## Devotional

Paul reveals what breaks his heart—and he doesn't describe it with anger. He describes it with tears.

Lost people walking as enemies of the cross. People whose end is destruction, whose god is their appetite, whose minds are set on earthly things. These are not people Paul dismisses with religious superiority. These are people he weeps over.

And that's the heart God wants to develop in us through our pursuit of Him.

Your pursuit isn't meant to terminate on yourself. Spiritual disciplines aren't just for your personal benefit. Transformation has a target beyond you. The fast, the surrender, the pressing forward—all of it is shaping you into someone who can overflow into the lives of others.

These people—the ones Paul wept over—are everywhere. They're your neighbors. Your coworkers. Your family members. Your friends. And we walk past them every day, absorbed in our own growth, focused on our own ground, pursuing our own prize.

Paul wept because he understood what was at stake. John 3:16 reflects the same heart: God loved the world and gave. He pursued, He sacrificed, He sent. And now He sends us.

The mission is clear: know Christ, pursue the lost, and present them mature.

You don't need a script. You need a name. Name one person you are pursuing for Christ this year. Pray for them. Reach toward them. Watch what happens to your own purpose when your pursuit is for someone else.

Start small. Stay steady. Watch God work.

The fast ends. The journey continues. Surrender your gains. Rest in grace. Set your face forward. And weep for the lost.

The goal awaits. The prize is clear. The mission is defined.

## Application

1. Who in your life is living as an enemy of the cross that you could begin to pursue with prayer and intentionality?
2. How does knowing that your pursuit of Christ is meant to overflow to others change the way you approach spiritual growth?

## Challenge

Write down one person's name you want to pursue for Christ this year. Commit to daily prayer for them and identify one opportunity this week to move toward them.

## Prayer

Lord, give me tears for the lost. Help me to see people the way You see them. I don't want my pursuit of You to end with me. I want it to overflow into the lives of others. Show me who You're calling me to reach. Give me courage to take steps toward them. Use everything You've done in me to draw someone else to You. In Jesus' name, amen.
