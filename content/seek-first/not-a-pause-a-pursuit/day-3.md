## Devotional

Our pursuit is not an effort to gain something from God. It's our response to Christ pursuing us first.

Paul makes the sequence explicit. Christ made him His own before Paul pursued. Rescue precedes response. The embrace comes before the effort. This is not a small distinction. It is the foundation everything else is built on.

Many believers treat spiritual disciplines like a ladder. Fast to get God's attention. Pray to earn His favor. Serve to accumulate standing. This approach exhausts because it rests on the wrong foundation. You're running toward someone you're not sure will accept you when you arrive.

But when pursuit is fueled by God's prior pursuit of you, everything transforms.

Effort becomes gratitude. Discipline becomes devotion. Striving becomes rest.

Burning out in spiritual pursuit happens when you're running to earn something. Discouragement sets in when results don't meet expectations. Comparison breeds failure when you measure your progress against others. But when you understand that you're responding to Someone who already ran toward you—who already reached down when you couldn't reach up—the whole posture changes.

Romans 5:8 demonstrates it: Christ died for sinners. He ran toward the people running away. He made family out of enemies. He pursued first.

Heart posture determines sustainability. Running from obligation depletes your fuel. Running from love runs indefinitely.

If you are unfamiliar with God's pursuit of you, know this: He is drawing you and inviting you right now.

## Application

1. In what ways have you treated your spiritual disciplines more like earning than responding?
2. How does knowing that Christ pursued you first change your motivation for pursuing Him?

## Challenge

Spend five minutes thanking God for pursuing you—without any requests or confessions. Simply express gratitude for His pursuit.

## Prayer

Jesus, thank You for pursuing me. Thank You for not waiting until I was worthy. Thank You for reaching down when I couldn't reach up. I want my pursuit of You to be a response to Your love, not an attempt to earn it. Free me from striving and let me rest in what You've already done. I'm Yours because You made me Yours. In Your name, amen.
