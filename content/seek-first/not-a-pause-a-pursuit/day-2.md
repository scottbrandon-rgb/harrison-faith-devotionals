## Devotional

What you cling to reveals what you worship.

Paul had a resume most people would envy. Circumcised on the eighth day. From the tribe of Benjamin. A Hebrew of Hebrews. A Pharisee. Blameless under the law. These weren't small credentials. They were the foundation of his identity, his standing, his worth.

And he called all of it garbage.

Not because accomplishment is sinful. Not because those things weren't real. But because he found something of surpassing worth, and by comparison, everything else lost its hold. Knowing Christ Jesus as Lord changed how he valued everything else.

Here's the question that makes this personal. What provides your significance apart from Christ? What accomplishments give you worth? What comforts sustain you when difficulty arrives? Most of us know exactly what those things are—we reach for them when we're anxious, bored, or feeling small.

Paul's instruction is clear: count it all as loss. Not because enjoyment or achievement are wrong, but because nothing compares to knowing Christ Jesus as Lord.

Genuine pursuit requires subtraction. Before you can run toward what matters most, you have to name and release what's been competing for that place. Progress requires traveling light. You can't sprint toward Christ while clutching everything that offered what only He can give.

And here's the grace in it: Paul's gains weren't evil. His tribe, his religious standing, his law-keeping were commendable things. They just built identity on the wrong foundation. When he surrendered them, he didn't lose anything of real value. In Christ, he found the acceptance, the accomplishment, and the identity he had been seeking everywhere else.

The path forward is the same. Name the substitutes. Surrender them consciously. Count them as loss compared to knowing God.

## Application

1. What are the "gains" in your life that you've been tempted to cling to more than Christ?
2. How has confidence in your own effort or performance hindered your pursuit of knowing Jesus more deeply?

## Challenge

Create a list of three things that once defined your sense of worth or accomplishment. Pray over each one, asking God to help you count them as loss compared to knowing Him.

## Prayer

Lord, I confess that I've held on to things that compete with You. I've found my worth in accomplishments and comforts and approval that don't come from You. Today I want to surrender my gains. Help me to see them as Paul did—as loss compared to the surpassing worth of knowing You. I don't want substitutes anymore. I want You. In Jesus' name, amen.
