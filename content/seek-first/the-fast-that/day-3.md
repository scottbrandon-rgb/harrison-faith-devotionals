## Devotional

Fasting trains you to live with open hands.

Every element God describes as genuine fasting involves releasing, giving, and extending. Loosing bonds. Sharing bread. Covering the naked. Welcoming the homeless. True fasting doesn't make you a keeper. It shapes you into a giver.

When you fast to gain, you clutch tightly. You're essentially telling God, "I gave something up, now You owe me something back." That's religious commerce. That's spiritual transaction. But when you fast to become, you practice release. You train your heart away from grasping and toward giving. An open hand is the posture of the kingdom.

Here's the contrast. Graspers ask when they can eat again. Givers ask who they can bless while going without. Graspers count the days until the fast ends. Givers count the opportunities during it. Graspers seek personal benefit. Givers seek the widow, the orphan, and the one with nothing.

Kingdom mathematics operates counterintuitively. You pour out and God fills up. You empty yourself and God satisfies you. The world says hoard what you have. The kingdom says release brings abundance.

In Leviticus 25, God established the Year of Jubilee. Debts forgiven. Slaves freed. Fields returned. The reason? Freedom, provision, and security come from God alone—not from what you hold onto. But Israel received grace and refused to extend it. They kept others in bondage while enjoying freedom themselves.

True fasting is not merely abstaining. It's actively giving away. Denying yourself so someone else can have more. That's the fast God chooses.

## Application

1. What are you currently holding onto that God might be asking you to release?
2. Who in your life needs you to extend grace to them this week?

## Challenge

Find one practical way today to give something away—time, money, attention, or help—as a practice of open-handed generosity.

## Prayer

Father, I confess that my natural posture is to clutch rather than release. I hold onto what I have because I fear not having enough. Today I ask You to train my hands to open. Show me someone I can bless. Show me something I can give away. Teach me the kingdom math where pouring out leads to filling up. Make me a giver rather than a grasper. In Jesus' name, amen.
