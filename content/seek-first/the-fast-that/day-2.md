## Devotional

Listen to the language Israel used when they complained to God. "Why have WE fasted and YOU see it not? Why have WE humbled OURselves and YOU take no knowledge of it?"

The emphasis is entirely on self. We did this. You owe us that. Their fasting was not an offering. It was a transaction. They gave something up expecting something in return, and when God didn't deliver, they were indignant.

God's response was to point to their behavior as evidence. On the very days they fasted, they pursued personal pleasure, oppressed their workers, quarreled, fought, and used force to control outcomes. They were fasting with clenched fists. Restricting their diet while tightening their grip on everything else.

Here's the problem with transactional faith. When faith becomes self-worship rather than God-worship, it manifests as indifference toward others. You can't genuinely fast and oppress. You can't truly humble yourself and exploit. The two are incompatible.

A fast that acquires asks: What will I gain? A fast that transforms asks: Who will I become?

Israel treated fasting like religious commerce. They made a deposit expecting a withdrawal. But God is not a vending machine. He is not interested in distributing material rewards in exchange for spiritual disciplines. His interest is in shaping you into the image of Christ.

This transactional mindset is everywhere in modern faith. People fast expecting breakthrough. They pray anticipating answers on a timeline. They give expecting returns. But spiritual disciplines don't manipulate divine favor. They reshape character.

The question transforms everything. Not "what will I receive from this fast?" but "who will I become through it?"

## Application

1. When you pray or fast, are you primarily focused on what you will receive or who you are becoming?
2. How has a transactional view of God affected your expectations of Him?

## Challenge

Rewrite your prayer requests for today. Replace "give me" with "make me."

## Prayer

Lord, I confess that I've approached You transactionally. I have fasted for breakthrough, prayed for blessings, and given expecting returns. I surrender my acquisition agenda and ask instead for transformation. Shape me into someone who looks more like Jesus. I trust that becoming who You want me to be will put everything else in its right place. In Jesus' name, amen.
