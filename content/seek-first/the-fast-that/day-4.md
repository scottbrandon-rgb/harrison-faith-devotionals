## Devotional

Jesus had every right to grasp. He was in the form of God. Equality with God was not something He was reaching for—it was already His. And yet He refused to exploit it.

The Greek word Paul uses here is harpagmos—it means to seize or clutch for personal advantage. Jesus refused to do that. Instead of retaining His privileges, He relinquished them. He poured Himself out for people who did not deserve it. He took the form of a servant when He had every right to the throne.

Paul reinforces this in 2 Corinthians 8, describing how Christ became poor so that through His poverty, we could become rich. Romans 5 puts it even more plainly: God demonstrated His love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. Not for the righteous. Not for the deserving. For us.

That's what the fast is forming in you.

True fasting cultivates Christlike character. It's about becoming like the One who released rather than grasped, who poured Himself out for the hungry and the lost. When you practice letting go during a fast—of food, of control, of acquisition—you are rehearsing the posture of Jesus.

The objective of this fast transcends food abstinence. It is spiritual formation. It is becoming someone who reflects the generosity, the sacrifice, and the love of Christ.

What you withhold from your flesh, you offer to the Spirit. What you release from your hands, you place in His.

## Application

1. In what specific area of your life is God asking you to empty yourself like Jesus did?
2. How does knowing that Christ gave to you while you were still His enemy change how you view giving to others?

## Challenge

Identify one privilege or right you have been clutching—the right to comfort, to understanding, to priority—and consciously release it today as a practice in becoming more Christlike.

## Prayer

Jesus, You had every right to hold onto Your privileges, but You released them for me. You did not grasp what was Yours. You gave it away so that I could receive what I did not deserve. Today I want to become more like You. Form Your character in me. Teach me to empty myself just like You did. Let my life reflect Your generosity, Your sacrifice, and Your love. Make me a living picture of who You are. In Your name, amen.
