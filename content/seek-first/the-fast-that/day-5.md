## Devotional

The fast that fails produces nothing. The fast that forms produces character. The fast that frees produces a future.

God makes extraordinary promises to the one who learns to release and stop grasping. Your light breaks forth like dawn. Healing springs up speedily. Righteousness goes before you. The Lord answers your call. He says, "Here I am."

But those promises hinge on a posture. Stop grasping and start giving. Pour yourself out.

And when you do, look at what God says happens. Ancient ruins are rebuilt. Foundations are raised for future generations. You are called the repairer of the breach, the restorer of streets to dwell in.

That's not just personal blessing. That's generational legacy.

Harrison Faith is called to rebuild altars in families. To raise foundations that future generations will stand on. To repair the cultural breaches that have fractured our communities. To restore neighborhoods and households and lives. That work begins with us. It begins with households of faith that have been genuinely transformed by fasting that's real.

Stop counting what you gain and start asking who you become. When you become open-handed and grace-extending and Christlike, Isaiah's promises stop being abstract and start becoming your story.

The fast that fails focuses on self. The fast that forms focuses on Christ. The fast that frees focuses on others.

## Application

1. What "ruins" in your life or family is God calling you to rebuild through becoming more like Christ?
2. How can you be a repairer of the breach in your home, workplace, or community this week?

## Challenge

Write down one specific area where you will commit to being a restorer rather than a consumer. Identify the ruins, name the breach, and ask God how your transformed life can bring rebuilding to that place.

## Prayer

Father, I want to be part of Your rebuilding work. I don't want a fast that fails or even just a fast that forms me. I want a fast that frees—that produces a future for my family, my church, and my city. Use me as a repairer of the breach and a restorer of streets. Let my open hands, my generous heart, and my Christlike character become the foundation for generations. I am ready to be poured out so that others can be filled up. In Jesus' name, amen.
