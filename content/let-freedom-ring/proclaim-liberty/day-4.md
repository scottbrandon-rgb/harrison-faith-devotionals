## Devotional

I would like to believe I am the same man when no one is watching. Some days I know better. The gap between the watched life and the unwatched life has a name, and our founders staked an entire nation on closing it.

In 1798, John Adams wrote a line every generation of Americans has had to reckon with: Our Constitution was made only for a moral and religious people. It is wholly inadequate to the government of any other. Read that the way he meant it. The governing document of our nation was written by a people of virtue, for a people of virtue, and it can only be maintained by a people of virtue. Adams understood that no parchment restrains a corrupt heart. Freedom cannot outlive the character of the people who hold it.

Which raises the question the whole experiment hangs on. What produces virtuous people? What produces the kind of character that acts in integrity and accountability when no one is looking?

Peter answers with a chain. Make every effort to supplement your faith with virtue, and virtue with knowledge, and knowledge with self-control, and self-control with steadfastness, and steadfastness with godliness, and godliness with brotherly affection, and brotherly affection with love (2 Peter 1:5-7). Look at where the chain starts. Faith comes first, and virtue hangs from it, because Peter says God's divine power has already granted us everything that pertains to life and godliness (2 Peter 1:3). Virtue grows out of a nature God grants, and only one person hands out new natures. Only Christ redeems a man. Only Christ renews a man. Only Christ gives him a mind, body, and soul that would think of others before himself. Law can restrain evil, and history proves law alone has never made a single man good. Constantine transformed Rome with rules. Christianity transformed Rome through reformation, one heart at a time.

So the health of our freedom traces back, root to fruit, to the health of the gospel among the people who hold it. That should land close to home, because virtue for a nation begins as virtue for a Tuesday afternoon. The unwatched moment. The tax form. The browser. The conversation your spouse will never hear about. Character built there, by grace, through faith supplemented daily, becomes the kind of people Adams said the whole thing was made for.

Freedom needs virtue, and virtue needs the gospel, so freedom traced all the way down needs a church willing to guard the gospel and give it away. Paul wrote one sentence that gathers this whole week into a charge. Tomorrow we finish with what our freedom is actually for.

## Application

1. Where does your character run thinnest when no one is watching?
2. Which link in Peter's chain needs your effort this week, and what will supplementing it look like on your actual calendar?

## Challenge

Pick the one unwatched place where your integrity bends, and put a witness there today. Tell a trusted brother or sister exactly where you are weakest and ask them to check on you this week. Virtue grows fastest where accountability takes root.

## Prayer

Father, Your divine power has granted me everything that pertains to life and godliness, and still I try to manufacture virtue out of willpower. Forgive me. Redeem the man I am when no one is watching. Renew my nature until integrity stops being a performance and starts being a reflection of Christ in me. Supplement my faith, Lord, link by link, until love holds the whole chain together. Amen.
