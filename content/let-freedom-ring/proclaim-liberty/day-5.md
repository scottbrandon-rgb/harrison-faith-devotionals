## Devotional

Two hundred fifty years ago, our founders attempted a form of government no nation other than Israel had ever carried off. A people governing themselves. We have spent four days walking that road, from jubilee trumpets to a nation without a king to a compact signed in a ship's cabin to the virtue Adams said the whole experiment required. Here is the question that set my heart ablaze through all of it. What does the church need beyond a history lesson?

Because this nation was birthed by the church. The conviction to leave England and worship without corruption came from the church. The earliest frameworks of governance came from congregations reading their Bibles. Days of fasting and prayer preceded the convention that ratified our Constitution. When the colonists of Connecticut wrote out the purpose of their government, they said it plainly, that they joined themselves into one commonwealth to maintain and preserve the liberty and purity of the Gospel of our Lord Jesus which we now profess. That single line carries the weight of everything. Liberty, so the gospel could be proclaimed freely. Purity, so the gospel being proclaimed would still be the gospel. Our framework of government was built as a hedge around those two words.

George Washington confirmed it. When 15 denominations wrote the new president asking whether he would choose a national religion, he answered that no one would be more zealous than himself to build barriers against spiritual tyranny, and that every man conducting himself as a good citizen ought to be protected in worshipping God according to the dictates of his own conscience. The government would guard the garden. The church would grow the tree.

Remember the tree. Israel governed itself like a living organism, every root feeding the whole, and we saw on Wednesday that a nation grows the same way. Now hear the warning on the other side of that picture. What becomes of our nation if the gospel is diluted? What becomes of a people when the church grows quiet, when the roots are starved of the very thing that gives the tree its life? The same thing that becomes of any tree cut off at the roots. It stands for a while on the strength of old wood. Then it falls, and everyone acts surprised.

So we arrive at 250 years, and I want us to say why out loud. 250, by the grace of God. 250, by the gospel of Jesus Christ. 250, by the church's willingness to proclaim and preserve what God used to begin it.

Which brings us to Paul, and to the sentence we have carried in our memory all week. For you were called to freedom, brothers. Only do not use your freedom as an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another (Galatians 5:13). Freedom always gets spent on something. That is its nature. Liberty is a wide open account, and every day you and I draw it down on the flesh or draw it down on love. Paul says the whole law is fulfilled in one word: You shall love your neighbor as yourself (Galatians 5:14). The freedom our grandfathers bled for, the freedom the pilgrims crossed an ocean for, the freedom Christ died to give, all of it was purchased so that love would have room to move.

So let freedom ring. Let it ring the way it was meant to, from a church that guards the purity of the gospel with one hand and pours out the liberty of it with the other. My great, great, great grandfather hoped for a freedom he never saw. I intend to spend the one I was handed. Serve somebody with it this week, deliberately and concretely, and the trumpet of jubilee will sound one more time, in a kitchen, on a porch, across a fence line, wherever love shows up carrying your name.

## Application

1. When you look honestly at the past month, what has your freedom mostly been spent on?
2. Who is one neighbor you will serve before this weekend ends, and what specifically will you do for them?

## Challenge

Spend some freedom on love before Sunday. Pick one person, a neighbor, a widow, a worn out parent, somebody who cannot repay you, and serve them in a way that costs you something real. Time, money, sweat, pride, any of it. Then tell no one except the Lord. Freedom spent on love never runs out.

## Prayer

Father, You called me to freedom and paid for it with Your Son. Forgive me for every day I have spent that freedom on my flesh and called it my right. Set my liberty to work in love. Give me eyes for the neighbor in front of me, hands willing to serve without applause, and a heart that guards the purity of Your gospel while giving away its liberty. Let freedom ring through this church, through my home, and through me, until You return or You call me home. Amen.
