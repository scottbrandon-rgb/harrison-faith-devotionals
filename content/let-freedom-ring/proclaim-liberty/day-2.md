## Devotional

For six thousand years the story of human government read like one long rerun. Power pooled. Pharaohs, emperors, caesars, czars, sultans, khans, the titles changed with the borders and the centuries, and the ending never did. Authority ran uphill until it collected in the hands of one man.

It started early. The first consolidated government Scripture records belonged to Nimrod, and Jewish commentary says the tower of Babel doubled as his insurance policy. If God ever flooded the world again, the ruler would survive on top. The people below could fend for themselves. That tower also dug in its heels against the first command God ever gave, to be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth, so God confused their languages and scattered them across the world (Genesis 11:8). Top down government was born in defiance of God, and it held the field for millennia.

Then came the anomaly. One nation in the ancient world was ruled without a king. In Israel, every person, male and female, was made in the image of the Creator (Genesis 1:27), carrying rights no government granted and no government could take away. Families held permanent title to their land. Every citizen was taught to read, something no other nation in history had attempted. The poor were cared for because everyone gave. The highest authority in the land was the Torah, and since God gave it, no man had the power to rewrite it.

Even when Israel later demanded kings, God fenced the throne with His Word. The king was required to write out a copy of the law with his own hand, keep it beside him, and read it all the days of his life, so that his heart would not be lifted up above his brothers (Deuteronomy 17:18-20). The most powerful man in the nation bent over a desk, copying Scripture line by line, because even a king needed something above him.

That is the question the anomaly puts to every one of us. What sits above you? Every heart runs like a little nation, and power pools in hearts the same way it pools in empires. Give self the throne and self will build towers. The king's remedy is still the remedy. Put your heart under the Word daily, deliberately, in your own handwriting if that is what it takes, until the Word governs what the throne room of your chest keeps trying to seize.

A people governed by God from the inside would eventually reshape how nations were governed on the outside. Centuries later, a group of scholars dusted off Israel's forgotten model, and a congregation boarded a ship. Tomorrow we meet the called out.

## Application

1. Where in your life has your heart been quietly lifted up above your brothers, convinced it answers to no one?
2. Who has God placed in your life with the standing to question you, and when will you invite them to actually do it?

## Challenge

Do what the kings of Israel were commanded to do. Take a pen and copy Deuteronomy 17:18 through 20 by hand today. Write slowly. Let every line remind you that the throne of your heart sits under a higher law.

## Prayer

Father, You are the authority above every authority, and Your Word stands when every tower falls. Search my heart for the places where power has pooled, where I have crowned myself and called it conviction. Lower my heart back under Your law. Give me the habit of the king You described, Your Word copied into my days until it is copied into my nature. Rule me first, Lord, so that nothing else has to. Amen.
