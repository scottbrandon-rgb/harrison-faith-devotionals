## Devotional

I think we understand by now that your heart's condition affects everything. It shapes your reactions, your desires, your habits, your ... worship. It also shapes your _vision_. If your heart is crowded, distracted, or weighed down, it will blur how you see God; that's important to understand and not just nod your head to.

Jesus said the pure in heart will see God -- not just know about Him, not just hear sermons about Him, but actually see Him moving in your life. That means the heart has to be cleared out, not just cleaned up.

And listen to this: Jesus didn't say "blessed are the pure in hands," as if the focus was only on what we do. He didn't say "pure in body," like it's just about physical purity. He didn't say "pure in mouth," as if speech alone was the key. He went deeper. He said "pure in heart."

Because we can have clean hands but bitter motives.

We can say the right words and still be polluted on the inside.

We can go through the motions and still have a heart that's hiding idolatry, jealousy, or fear.

That's why the heart matters most, because it's the control center of your life, and the lens through which you see God.

Proverbs says, "Guard your heart above all else…" Why? Because the heart determines your vision. If it's full of unforgiveness, cluttered with anxiety, distracted by approval, or tangled in sin, your spiritual sight will be distorted.

If your heart is divided, you will struggle to see God clearly. But if it's surrendered, single-minded, and purified, then you'll start seeing Him everywhere: in the conversation, in the hardship, in the waiting, in the breakthrough, and in the rescue.

Imagine opening your heart like a cluttered room and saying, "Lord, clean out what doesn't belong I want to see You again." That's where clarity begins. It reminds me of a song we used to sing.

The more I seek You

The more I find You

The more I find You

The more I love You

Seek Him today, and you will see Him today.

## Application

1. What’s cluttering your heart right now — emotionally, mentally, or spiritually? Are there areas where your actions look clean, but your heart needs attention? How can you begin guarding your heart more intentionally this week?

## Challenge

Take inventory of your heart today.Ask: “Am I focused on cleaning the outside while ignoring what’s going on inside?” Write down anything the Spirit reveals, and ask Him to purify your heart.

## Prayer

Father, I don’t want a life that just looks right on the surface. I want purity where it matters most — in my heart. Search me. Show me what’s clouding my vision. Forgive me for focusing on external things when You’re after something deeper. Purify me from the inside out, so I can see You more clearly. In Jesus’ name, Amen.
