## Devotional

Jesus makes something clear...if you truly belong to Him, the world will notice—and it won’t always applaud. In John 15, He tells His disciples that they don’t belong to the world anymore. And that shift in identity comes with a shift in treatment.

Now, the world is okay with religion as long as it stays comfortable, convenient, and silent. But the moment righteousness speaks up… the moment light exposes darkness… the moment truth confronts the popular lie…yeah, get ready for the pushback.

And here’s what that means for you, if you’re experiencing tension, if people are distancing themselves and you’re losing favor not because you’re rude or reckless, but because you’re aligned with Jesus—then you’re feeling the same pressure He felt.

If the world never pushes back against your life, then maybe it doesn’t see much difference in it. But if your walk with Christ creates friction with the values of the world, then you’re walking in truth.

Jesus isn’t surprised by the world’s hostility. He expects it. And He prepares us for it—not to live in fear, but to walk in faith. You don’t need to be angry, defensive, or bitter. You just need to be faithful.

Because the more you resemble Him, the more resistance you may feel. But that resistance is a reminder: you’re not who you used to be—and you don’t belong where you used to live.

## Application

1. Have you ever softened your convictions to avoid pushback? In what areas is God calling you to walk in courage instead of compromise? How can you respond to resistance with faith and gentleness?

## Challenge

Boldly live out one conviction today that you’ve been tempted to hide. Say aloud: “Jesus, I’ll walk in truth—even when the world pushes back.”

## Prayer

Lord, when the world pushes back, help me not to flinch. Teach me to walk in love and truth without fear. I don’t want to blend in—I want to stand out for You. Give me a heart that isn’t bitter, a voice that isn’t silent, and a life that clearly points to the kingdom. In Jesus’ name, Amen.
