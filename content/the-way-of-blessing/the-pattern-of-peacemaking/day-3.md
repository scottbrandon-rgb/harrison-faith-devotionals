## Devotional

There’s a reason peace feels like a battle in the home—it’s because the enemy attacks where God designed harmony. And one of the most contested battlegrounds is this: a wife’s role in submission to God's design.

Let’s be honest—submission has been distorted by culture, misused by some, and misunderstood by many. But in God's economy, submission is not about silence, weakness, or inferiority. It’s about alignment. It’s trusting the structure God put in place so that peace can flow.

God’s order isn’t oppressive—it’s protective. It doesn’t squash your voice—it strengthens your role. But when that order is resisted—whether in rebellion, bitterness, or pride—peace gets choked out at the root.

If…then. If a wife aligns herself under God’s design, then peace flows—not just to her, but through her, into the home. But if she resists the structure and calls it “strength,” what feels empowering in the moment often breeds long-term unrest.

Peter doesn’t tell wives to disappear—he tells them to carry a spirit that is gentle, quiet, and powerful. This isn’t passivity—it’s a posture that trusts God enough to obey even when it’s difficult.

Wives: this kind of alignment isn’t easy. It doesn’t always feel fair. But it’s faith-filled, and that’s what makes it holy. Your submission isn’t to a flawed man—it’s to a perfect God who sees your heart and honors your obedience.

## Application

1. In what ways have you struggled with God’s design for your role as a wife? What lies have you believed about submission that need to be replaced with truth? What step of realignment can you take today that will open the way for peace to flow?

## Challenge

Ask the Holy Spirit to reveal any resistance in your heart toward God's structure for your role. Write down what He shows you. Then pray for the courage to release control and walk in trust.

## Prayer

Father, You are a God of order, not confusion. I confess that at times I’ve resisted the role You’ve called me to, thinking it was limiting instead of life-giving. Teach me to trust You by walking in the structure You designed. Help me submit not out of duty, but from devotion. Let peace flow in my home as I align myself with Your way. In Jesus’ name, Amen.
