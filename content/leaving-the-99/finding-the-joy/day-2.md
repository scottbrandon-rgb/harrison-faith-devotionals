Title
What Makes Heaven Shout

Excerpt
"What causes heaven to be loud with the singing of the Lord? Those who have been saved by the mighty one."

Scripture Reading
Zephaniah 3:17 ESV
"The LORD your God is in your midst, a mighty one who will save; he will rejoice over you with gladness; he will quiet you by his love; he will exult over you with loud singing."

Devotional Thought

If God is the source of all joy, then we have to ask a question that most of us don't think to ask. What makes God rejoice? Not what makes us feel joyful, not what makes Sunday mornings feel good, but what stirs up joy in the heart of God Himself?

The answer runs like a thread through the entire Old Testament, and once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

In Deuteronomy 30, God already knows Israel will fail. He knows they will choose disobedience. But He looks ahead to the day when they wake up and realize they need Him, and He says something incredible. "The LORD will again rejoice over you for good, just as He rejoiced over your fathers." Not anger first. Not punishment first. Rejoicing. God's response to a repentant people is joy.

In Isaiah 62, God looks forward to the salvation of His people and He will not be quiet about it. He describes it like a bridegroom on his wedding day. "As the bridegroom rejoices over the bride, so shall your God rejoice over you." Just think about that for a second. The most personal, intimate, overwhelming joy a person can experience... God says that is what He feels over His people coming home.

In Jeremiah 32, the Lord says He will rejoice over them to do them good and will faithfully plant them in the land "with all My heart and with all My soul." That phrase should stop you in your tracks, because those are the same words God requires of us when He says to love Him with all your heart and soul. So what causes God to pour out every bit of who He is? Returning His lost people.

And then there is Zephaniah. The Lord calls Jerusalem rebellious, defiled, a city that listens to no voice and accepts no correction. But He looks forward to a remnant, humble and lowly, who will seek refuge in His name. And when that day comes, the Lord your God will be in your midst, a mighty one who will save. He will rejoice over you with gladness. He will quiet you by His love. He will exult over you with loud singing.

So what I'm seeing is this. From Deuteronomy to Zephaniah, the thing that makes heaven loud is not our performance, not our programs, not how well we kept the rules. It is restoration. Lost people coming home. Every single time God describes His own joy, it is connected to sinners returning to Him. Not to anything they did to earn it, but to the fact that they came back at all.

And if God's greatest joy is found in the restoration of lost people, then the Spirit living inside of you carries that same joy. The question is whether we have given it room to breathe or buried it under everything else we think the Christian life is supposed to be about.

Tomorrow we will look at the moment when that joy was tested to its absolute limit, and what Jesus saw when He looked at the cross.

Application Questions
1. When you consider that God's joy is consistently tied to restoring lost people throughout scripture, how does that reshape the way you think about your own role in reaching the lost?
2. Zephaniah 3:17 says God will quiet you by His love and exult over you with loud singing. When was the last time you let the reality of God's joy over you actually settle into your heart?

Today's Challenge
Pick one of the four passages mentioned today (Deuteronomy 30:9, Isaiah 62:5, Jeremiah 32:41, or Zephaniah 3:17) and read it in its full context. Ask the Lord to show you what His joy looks like when it is directed at someone coming home.

Prayer to Pray
Lord, I am amazed that Your greatest joy is not found in my performance but in my return. You rejoice like a bridegroom, You shout with loud singing, You pour out all Your heart and soul over people coming home to You. Let that truth reshape the way I see the lost. If restoration is what makes You rejoice, then let it be what stirs my heart too. Forgive me for the times I have been more passionate about my comfort than about Your celebration. In Jesus' name, amen.
